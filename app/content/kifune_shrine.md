According to legend, Princess Tamayori-hime, the mother of the first Emperor Jimmu, traveled up the river from Osaka Bay in a yellow boat (kifune). ### 2. ğŸ® A Stroll Through the Grounds: Red Lanterns and Three Sanctuaries

Kifune Shrine is divided into three areas: the Hongu (Main Shrine), the Nakamiya (Yui-no-yashiro), and the Okumiya (Inner Shrine). Take a slow walk, following the sound of the water.

#### **The Red Lantern Staircase to the Hongu**

After getting off at the bus stop and walking a short distance, you'll see the iconic stone staircase of Kifune Shrine. The red Kasuga lanterns lining both sides create a dreamlike atmosphere against the backdrop of green maple trees. It is beautiful during the day, but when the lights are turned on in the evening, it feels as if you are entering the world of the gods. This is the perfect spot for an unforgettable photo.

#### **The Mystical Water Fortune, Mizu-ura Mikuji**

An absolute must-do when you reach the Hongu! It's the **'water fortune-telling'**. Take a blank white slip of paper and gently float it on the sacred water (Goshinsui) next to the main hall. As the paper gets wet, letters will slowly appear, revealing your fortune. Your heart will flutter the moment you see the message from the water god. (You can also scan a QR code to see a translation in English!)

#### **Yui-no-yashiro (Nakamiya), a Shrine for Tying Bonds**

A little further up from the Hongu is **Yui-no-yashiro**, which enshrines the god of 'matchmaking (enmusubi)'. It is famous for the legend of the renowned Heian period poet, Izumi Shikibu, who prayed here and rekindled her husband's love. It is said that writing your wish on a green slip of paper and tying it here will bring you a good match, so those dreaming of love should be sure to visit.

#### **The Sacred Forest, Okumiya (Inner Shrine)**

Located in the innermost part of the grounds, the Okumiya is the birthplace of Kifune Shrine. It is said that the **'yellow boat'** that Princess Tamayori-hime rode is buried under a pile of stones beneath the main hall. Surrounded by a lush forest, this place has a quiet and solemn atmosphere where you can feel the energy of a true power spot.

### 4. ğŸƒ Nearby Attractions: Kyoto in Summer and Autumn

The area around Kifune Shrine boasts different charms each season.

#### **1. Kawadoko (Riverside Dining)**

- **Season**: May to September.
- **Features**: A classic Kyoto summer experience where you dine on platforms built directly over the Kibune River. Enjoying Kaiseki cuisine or Nagashi Somen (noodles flowing down a bamboo chute) while listening to the running water is a great way to forget the heat. It's like a feast for the gods!

#### **2. Hiking to Kurama-dera Temple**

- **Course**: From Kifune Shrine over the mountain to Kurama-dera Temple (approx. 1.5 hours).
- **Features**: A trek over Mount Kurama, steeped in the legends of the 'Tengu' goblin. You can feel the energy of nature firsthand while walking through the dense cedar forest and along the 'Path of Tree Roots'. If you're confident in your stamina, give it a try.

#### **3. Eizan Railway Maple Tunnel**

- **Season**: Mid to late November.
- **Features**: The Eizan Railway on the way to Kibune passes through a tunnel of maple trees in autumn. At night, you can have a fantastical experience riding through the illuminated foliage.
