His name itself evokes the energy of 'martial arts (æ­¦)' and 'thunder (é›·),' and he is the strongest god of swords (åŠç¥) and war in Japanese mythology.

#### **ã€Origin and History: The Protagonist of the 'Transfer of the Land' (Kuniyuzuri)ã€‘**

The history of Kashima Jingu is intertwined with the 'Transfer of the Land' story, a highlight of Japan's founding myths.
The sun goddess Amaterasu dispatched the mightiest martial god, Takemikazuchi, to pacify the terrestrial world. Descending to earth, he plunged his sword upside down into the ground, sat upon its hilt, and commanded the terrestrial deities, achieving the great feat of peacefully receiving the land. He later aided the first emperor, Emperor Jimmu, on his eastern expedition by sending down his divine sword (Futsunomitama), saving him from a crisis.

To honor these achievements, the shrine is said to have been founded here in 660 BCE, the first year of Emperor Jimmu's reign. Kashima Jingu is a place where the 'god who laid the foundations of the nation' resides, having been present since the very beginning of Japan.

### 2. ğŸŒ² Strolling the Precincts: Walking Through the Sacred Forest

The precincts of Kashima Jingu are a vast forest, equivalent to the size of 15 Tokyo Domes. This forest, home to over 600 species of plants, is designated as a Natural Monument, and simply walking through it allows you to feel an overwhelming life force.

#### **Otorii and Romon (Tower Gate)**

The pilgrimage begins by passing through the massive 'Otorii,' made from cedar trees felled in the forest. Following this is the brilliant red **'Romon' (æ¥¼é–€)**, a designated Important Cultural Property considered one of Japan's three great tower gates. You will be awed by its majesty as you step into the divine realm.

#### **Honden and Haiden: A Prayer for Decision**

The Main Hall (Honden) and Worship Hall (Haiden), donated by the second Tokugawa shogun, Hidetada, exude a solemn yet splendid grace. Stand before the Haiden here, pay your respects with "two bows, two claps, one bow," let go of your inner hesitations, and steel your resolve. The energy of the martial god will surely straighten your spine.

#### **Okusando (Inner Path) and Shikaen (Deer Park)**

The **'Okusando,'** a path leading deep into the forest past the main hall, is the highlight of Kashima Jingu. As you walk under the sunlight filtering through the dense canopy of giant trees that seem to touch the sky (komorebi), you will understand with your whole body why this is called a sacred forest.
Along the way is the **'Shikaen,'** where the divine messengers, the deer, live. The deer of Nara are also said to have originated from here, as legend tells that the god of Kashima traveled there riding a white deer, making the deer here a special presence.

#### **Kaname-ishi (Keystone) and Mitarashi-ike (Purification Pond)**

At the end of the forest path, you can find the legendary **'Kaname-ishi,'** a stone said to be embedded deep in the earth, pinning down the head of a giant catfish that causes earthquakes. Though it appears small on the surface, its roots are said to be immeasurably deep. Pray here to find your center in life and remain unshaken.
Further down is the **'Mitarashi-ike,'** a pond where 400,000 liters of clear water spring forth daily. The transparent water and tranquil atmosphere will bring peace to your mind.
