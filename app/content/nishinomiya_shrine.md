Before passing through the red gate of Nishinomiya Shrine, let's first hear the dramatic story of its master, Ebisu-sama.

The main deity (‰∏ªÁ•≠Á•û) here is **Ebisu-no-Okami (Hiruko-no-Mikoto)**.
Yes, that's him‚Äîthe one with a fishing rod in his right hand, a large sea bream in his left, and a broad, beaming smile. Originally a god of fishing prayed to by fishermen for a bountiful catch, he came to be revered as the **'God of Commerce'** and **'God of Wealth'** as trade and markets developed over time. Today, he is the guardian deity not only for business owners and merchants but for everyone who wishes for happiness and prosperity.

#### **„ÄêOrigins and History: The Grand Return of an Abandoned God„Äë**

The birth myth of Ebisu-sama is a little sad, yet hopeful. According to the Japanese creation myth, **Hiruko**, the first child of the deities Izanagi and Izanami, was born with a disability‚Äîhe had no bones. As a result, he was placed in a reed boat and cast adrift at sea.
However, he did not perish. He washed ashore here, off the coast of Nishinomiya (Wadanomisaki). The local fishermen enshrined him with great care, and in return, he became **'Ebisu,'** a god who brought fortune from across the sea, repaying the people for their kindness.

The story of Ebisu-sama, who overcame adversity to be reborn as the 'God of Fortune,' delivers a powerful and positive message to us all: "No matter the hardship, if you overcome it, good fortune will surely follow."

### 2. ‚õ©Ô∏è A Stroll Through the Precincts: The Red Gate and Unique Architecture that Beckon Fortune

Nishinomiya Shrine is a place where you can feel invigorated just by walking through its grounds. Here are the must-see spots.

#### **Akamon (Red Gate) and the Stone-Paved Path**

The main gate of the shrine, the **'Omotedaimon (Akamon),'** is a National Important Cultural Property. It is said to have been donated by Hideyori, the son of Toyotomi Hideyoshi, and it is the very gate where the 'Fukuotoko Erabi (Lucky Man Race)' begins at 6 AM on January 10th. The 230-meter path leading from the gate to the main hall is usually tranquil, but during the festival, it brims with the passionate energy of countless people.

#### **National Treasure Main Hall: The One-and-Only Ebisu-zukuri**

The main hall (Honden), located behind the worship hall (Haiden), is built in a unique architectural style known as **'Ebisu-zukuri (Ebisu style),'** the only one of its kind in Japan. Its magnificent structure, featuring three roofs connected in a row, is so historically valuable that it has been designated a National Treasure.

#### **Araebisu Shrine: The Aura of a Competitor**

In the southern part of the precincts, you will find the **'Araebisu Shrine,'** which enshrines the rough spirit (aramitama) of Ebisu-sama. Unlike the gentle Ebisu-sama, the deity here is said to provide a powerful drive when starting new ventures or facing a challenge. Those launching a business or facing an important contract should definitely pay a visit here.

### 4. ‚öæÔ∏è Nearby Attractions: The Sanctuary of Baseball and the Town of Sake

After visiting the shrine, enjoy some of Nishinomiya's other famous attractions.

#### **1. Hanshin Koshien Stadium**

- **Distance**: 1 stop by train (Hanshin Electric Railway).
- **Features**: The heart of Japanese baseball! It is the sacred ground of high school baseball and the home field of the Hanshin Tigers. On non-game days, you can visit the 'Koshien Museum' to feel the passionate spirit of Japanese baseball. A must-visit pilgrimage site for any baseball fan.

#### **2. Hakushika Memorial Sake Museum**

- **Distance**: 15-minute walk.
- **Features**: The 'Nada' region, where Nishinomiya is located, is Japan's top sake-producing area. At this museum, founded by a brewing family with 350 years of history, you can see old sake-brewing tools and taste freshly brewed sake. Don't forget to buy a bottle of sake as a souvenir.

#### **3. Patisserie Brothers (Dessert)**

- **Location**: 1st floor of the department store at Hanshin Nishinomiya Station.
- **Recommendation**: The square-shaped **'Cube Choux Cream'** is their specialty. Treat your tired body to a sweet break after all the walking.
