To understand the depth of Miho Shrine, it is essential to comprehend the special relationship and myth of the two deities enshrined here.

Two deities are enshrined here.

- **Mihotsuhime-no-mikoto**: The wife of ≈åkuninushi (the main deity of Izumo Taisha) and the goddess of agriculture. She is a benevolent mother goddess who governs the abundance of the five grains and the prosperity of descendants.
- **Kotoshironushi-no-kami (Ebisu-sama)**: The son of ≈åkuninushi, and the very same 'Ebisu-sama' we know well, holding a fishing rod and a sea bream. He is worshipped nationwide as the god of **fisheries, maritime safety, and business prosperity**. He is also the god of arts who loves **music and dance**.

Because these two deities are together, Miho Shrine is an all-purpose power spot where one can receive blessings for **family harmony**, **business success**, and **artistic inspiration**.

#### **„ÄêOrigin and History: The Moment of a Great Decision„Äë**

According to mythology, when the heavenly gods (led by Amaterasu) demanded that ≈åkuninushi, the ruler of the terrestrial world, hand over the land, ≈åkuninushi entrusted the decision to his son, Kotoshironushi.
At that time, Kotoshironushi, who was enjoying fishing in the sea off Mihonoseki, replied without hesitation, "It is right to offer this land to the descendants of the heavenly gods." He then overturned the boat he was on and hid himself within it.

Thanks to this sacrificial decision, a peaceful transfer of power occurred without war, and he became a symbol of keeping divine promises and a guardian of peace. Miho Shrine is the sacred site built on the very stage of this historic myth.

### 2. üåä Strolling the Precincts: A Sacred Realm of Sea and Music

Miho Shrine faces the sea, so the sound of waves can be heard from anywhere on the grounds. Experience the unique atmosphere of this place.

#### **Unique Architecture: Miho-zukuri (Áæé‰øùÈÄ†)**

If you look closely at the main hall (Honden), you will see a unique shape, as if two buildings are standing side-by-side with their arms around each other's shoulders. This is the only example in Japan of the **'Miho-zukuri'** style, with two connected sanctuaries enshrining Mihotsuhime (the mother) on the right and Kotoshironushi (the son) on the left. It exudes a sense of tranquility, like the image of a close mother and son.

#### **The Morning Ritual: Asamikesai (ÊúùÂæ°È•åÁ•≠)**

Every morning at 8:30 AM, a ritual is held where priests offer a meal to the deities. At this time, you can watch the elegant dance of the shrine maidens (miko) and listen to traditional musical instruments for free. The sound of the flute and drums cutting through the quiet morning air is a moving experience that seems to cleanse the soul. It's a privilege only early-rising travelers can enjoy!

#### **The Corridor and the Sea**

The corridor in front of the main hall is open on all sides, allowing the cool sea breeze to pass through. On days when raindrops fall, the ensemble of the sound of rain dripping from the eaves and the sound of the waves is truly music performed by nature. Perhaps this is the favorite sound of Ebisu-sama, the god of music?

In front of the shrine, there is a street that retains the atmosphere of the Edo period.

#### **1. Aoishidatami-d≈çri (Blue Cobblestone Street)**
