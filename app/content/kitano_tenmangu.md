### 1. üôè Deity and Origin: How a Tragic Genius Became the God of Learning

Behind the grandeur of Kitano Tenmangu lies the dramatic life and death of one man.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deity (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Sugawara no Michizane„Äë**

The principal deity here is **Sugawara no Michizane**, the greatest intellectual and politician of the Heian period.
Called a child prodigy from a young age, he rose to the rank of Minister of the Right, but was unjustly exiled to Dazaifu in Kyushu due to the slander of a political rival, where he died a lonely death. After his death, a series of natural disasters struck Kyoto, and to appease his vengeful spirit, people began to worship him as **'Tenjin (Â§©Á•û, a heavenly deity)'**.

Over time, Tenjin, once an object of fear, came to be revered as the **'God of Learning'** and the **'God of Intellect'** thanks to his outstanding scholarship. Today, it has become a symbol of hope, attracting a constant stream of visitors praying for success in various exams, including school entrance exams, certifications, and employment.

#### **„ÄêOrigin and History: A Faith Spanning a Millennium„Äë**

Founded in 947, Kitano Tenmangu has stood for over a thousand years as a guardian of Kyoto and a hall of learning.
It was also the site of the legendary Grand Kitano Tea Ceremony hosted by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, and its main hall, designated a National Treasure, showcases the essence of magnificent Momoyama-style architecture. The shrine grounds are filled with 1,500 plum trees, which Michizane cherished in his lifetime, transforming it into Kyoto's premier plum blossom viewing spot every February.

### 2. üêÇ A Stroll Through the Precincts: Petting Oxen and Searching for Stars

The precincts are filled with symbols related to Lord Michizane. Here are some key points you shouldn't miss.

#### **Nade-ushi (Stroking Ox): A Symbol of Wisdom and Healing**

Throughout the grounds, you will find various ox statues called **'Nade-ushi'**. This is because Lord Michizane was born in the Year of the Ox, and legend says his grave was built on the spot where the ox pulling his funeral cart stopped.
It is said that **"if you touch the part of the ox corresponding to your own ailment, it will be healed,"** and **"rubbing the ox's head is believed to make you smarter."** As a result, the heads of the oxen are always polished smooth. Find your favorite ox and make a wish as you stroke it gently.

#### **The National Treasure Main Hall and Ishinoma**

The main hall and worship hall, both National Treasures, are connected by an **'Ishinoma (stone room)'** in the unique 'Gongen-zukuri' style. The brilliant gold leaf decorations and complex roof curves are overwhelming to behold. Offer a prayer for academic success in front of the worship hall with "two bows, two claps, and one bow."

#### **Sankomon (Three Lights Gate) and the Hidden Star**

The middle gate, **'Sankomon,'** is so named because it is said to have carvings of the sun, moon, and stars. However, if you look closely, you can find carvings of the sun and moon, but not the star. Legend has it that this is because the North Star shone directly above the gate, so there was no need to carve it. (However, some rumors say there is a hidden star somewhere, so try to find it!)

#### **Plum Grove and Maple Garden**

From early February to late March, you can enjoy the subtle fragrance of plum blossoms in the **'Plum Grove (Bai-en),'** which is open to the public for a fee. In the fall, the **'Maple Garden (Momiji-en)'** opens, offering a spectacular view of a tunnel of red maples and the red Uguisubashi Bridge.

### 4. üçµ Nearby Attractions: The Tradition and Taste of Kyoto

After visiting the shrine, immerse yourself in the deep charms of Kyoto.

#### **1. Kamishichiken (Hanamachi)**

- **Location**: Right in front of the shrine's east gate.
- **Features**: This is the oldest geisha district in Kyoto. You can see traditional wooden buildings (ochaya) in an atmosphere more tranquil and serene than Gion. If you're lucky, you might even see a maiko.

#### **2. Toyouke Jaya (Tofu Cuisine)**

- **Location**: Across the street from the shrine's main gate.
- **Features**: A restaurant run by a tofu shop that's over 100 years old. The **'Toyouke-don (tofu rice bowl)'** is inexpensive yet boasts a deep flavor. It's a popular spot with lines, so try to go slightly before or after the main lunch hour.

#### **3. Kinkaku-ji (The Golden Pavilion)**

- **Access**: A 10-minute bus ride away.
- **Features**: A symbol of Kyoto, this temple shines with its golden pavilion. It's close to Kitano Tenmangu, making it an essential stop to visit together.
