The main deity (ä¸»ç¥­ç¥) here is **Hikohohodemi-no-Mikoto (also known as Yamasachihiko)**. He is a very important god, the son of the heavenly grandson Ninigi-no-Mikoto and the grandfather of Japan's first emperor, Emperor Jimmu.
Enshrined alongside him are his wife, **Toyotamahime-no-Mikoto** (daughter of the sea god), and **Shiotsuchi-no-Okami**, the god of wisdom who acted as their Cupid.

Because these three deities are gathered here, Aoshima Shrine is known to have powerful blessings for **'matchmaking (love)'**, **'marital harmony'**, **'safe childbirth'**, and **'maritime safety'**.

#### **ã€Origins and History: A Lost Fishhook and a Fateful Encounterã€‘**

The shrine's origins begin with the famous myth of 'Yamasachihiko and Umisachihiko.'
The younger brother, Yamasachihiko (the mountain hunter), borrows a fishhook from his older brother, Umisachihiko (the sea fisherman), goes out to sea, and loses it. As his brother rages, the god of wisdom, Shiotsuchi-no-Okami, appears and shows the distressed younger brother the way to the sea god's palace (the Dragon Palace).

Upon arriving at the Dragon Palace, Yamasachihiko meets the beautiful princess Toyotamahime, falls in love, and marries her. After three dream-like years, Yamasachihiko finds the lost fishhook and returns to the surface. The place where he is said to have built his palace and lived is this very **Aoshima**.

The tale has a poignant epilogue: Toyotamahime, heavily pregnant, later comes ashore to find her husband. However, during childbirth, her true form (a shark or a dragon) is revealed, and she returns to the sea in sorrow. Aoshima Shrine is a sacred site of myth that encompasses their meeting, love, and the pain of their separation.

The path to Aoshima Shrine is an adventure in itself.

#### **The Ogre's Washboard (é¬¼ã®æ´—æ¿¯æ¿)**

Look down from the 'Yayoi Bridge' leading to the island. A bizarre, wave-like rock formation that looks as if a giant used it as a washboard surrounds the entire island. This is a natural masterpiece, designated as a National Natural Monument, formed over 7 million years as geological strata were eroded by the waves. The vast spectacle revealed at low tide is overwhelmingly mystical.

#### **The Main Hall and Subtropical Plants**

Pass through the torii gate at the island's entrance, and the atmosphere changes completely. Over 5,000 Livistona palm trees form a tunnel, creating the illusion of being in a Southeast Asian jungle. Amidst the deep greenery, the vibrant vermilion **Main Hall (æœ¬æ®¿)** shines, boasting an exotic yet sacred beauty. Offer a sincere prayer here with the standard "two bows, two claps, and one bow."

#### **Motomiya (å…ƒå®®) and the Ancient Prayer Path**

Take the side path to the right of the main hall to find the true power spot. After passing through the 'Ancient Prayer Path' dense with palm trees, you will find the original site of Aoshima Shrine, the **'Motomiya (å…ƒå®®)'**. This is where ancient ritual artifacts were excavated, and it is filled with a primitive sanctity.
Here, you can have two special experiences.

- **Throwing Amanohiraka (Clay Discs)**: If you throw a small clay disc and it lands within the altar area, your wish will come true. If it breaks, it is said to ward off misfortune.
- **Tying Musubikoyori (Wishing Cords)**: Tie a colorful paper cord to the sacred 'Husband-and-Wife Palm Trees' while making a wish. (Pink: Romance / Purple: Health / Green: Academics, etc.)
