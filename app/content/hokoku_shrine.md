The energy of Hokoku Shrine is the very essence of its protagonist, Hideyoshi.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deity (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Toyotomi Hideyoshi„Äë**

The main deity (‰∏ªÁ•û, shushin) here is **Toyotomi Hideyoshi (Hokoku Daimyojin)**.
He is a legendary figure who started as Oda Nobunaga's sandal-bearer and rose to the position of Kampaku (equivalent to a prime minister). Thanks to his life of making the impossible possible, he is revered here as a god of **'career success'**, **'good fortune'**, and **'business prosperity'**.
His son, Hideyori, and his younger brother, Hidenaga, who are enshrined alongside him, are also seen as steadfast supporters protecting the family's prosperity.

#### **„ÄêOrigins and History: A Drama of Destruction and Revival„Äë**

After Hideyoshi's death, the original Hokoku Shrine, splendidly built in Kyoto, was completely destroyed by the Tokugawa shogunate. It was a dark age when even his name was taboo.
However, 250 years later, he was revived with the Meiji Restoration. The shrine was rebuilt in Nakanoshima, Osaka in 1879, and in 1961, it was moved inside Osaka Castle, the very place where he poured his lifelong dreams. The shrine's history of overcoming the storms of time and rising again is itself a symbol of **'revival'** and **'comeback'**.

### 2. üèØ A Stroll Through the Grounds: Gates and Statues that Beckon Success

The grounds are compact but filled with a powerful energy. Here are some key points you shouldn't miss.

#### **Statue of Hideyoshi and the Castle Tower**

Next to the worship hall stands a majestic statue of Hideyoshi holding a commander's baton. Taking a photo with the Osaka Castle tower in the background makes it feel as if he is encouraging you, saying, "You can do it too."

#### **Shiromidai (Castle Viewing Point)**

From **'Shiromidai'** inside the shrine, you can get a clear view of the castle tower through the trees. It's a hidden gem where you can make a wish while looking at Osaka Castle from one of the closest vantage points.

#### **The Gourd (Hyotan), a Symbol of Success**

You can see gourd motifs throughout the grounds. This is due to the legend of the **'Sennari Hyotan' (Thousand Gourds)**, where Hideyoshi would add a gourd to his banner after each victory in battle. The gourd is a direct symbol of victory and success.

### 4. ‚öîÔ∏è Nearby Attractions: Osaka's Past and Present

After visiting the shrine, take time to fully enjoy Osaka Castle Park.

#### **1. Osaka Castle Main Tower (Tenshukaku)**

- **Access**: 5-minute walk.
- **Features**: Osaka's iconic landmark that needs no introduction. Inside the museum, you can vividly experience the life of Hideyoshi and the history of the Summer Siege of Osaka. Feel what it's like to look down upon the world from the 8th-floor observatory.

#### **2. MIRAIZA OSAKA-JO**

- **Location**: Directly in front of the main tower.
- **Features**: A multi-purpose cultural facility housed in the remodeled former Army Division Headquarters building. With its classic atmosphere, cafes, restaurants, and souvenir shops, it's a great place to take a break. The view of the castle tower from the rooftop terrace is fantastic.

#### **3. Osaka Museum of History**

- **Access**: 15-minute walk (to the southwest of the park).
- **Features**: You can see the history of Osaka from ancient times to the modern day all in one place. The panoramic view of Osaka Castle Park from the 10th floor is a favorite photo spot for photographers.
