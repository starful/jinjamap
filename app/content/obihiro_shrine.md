### 1. üôè Deities and Origins: The Pioneer Gods Who Turned Wasteland into Fertile Soil

The roots of Obihiro Shrine are deeply connected to the history of Hokkaido's pioneering era.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deities (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): The Three Pioneer Gods„Äë**

This shrine enshrines three deities: **Okuninushi (nation-building)**, **Onamuchi (another name for Okuninushi)**, and **Sukunahikona (medicine and agriculture)**.
They are all mythological heroes who pioneered the wild lands and enriched people's lives. Therefore, Obihiro Shrine is believed to bring powerful blessings for **'business prosperity'**, **'bountiful harvests'**, **'household safety'**, and **'matchmaking (enmusubi)'**.

#### **„ÄêOrigins and History: Sowing the Seeds of Hope„Äë**

In 1883, the 'Banseisha' pioneer group, led by Benzo Yoda from Shizuoka, took their first steps in Obihiro. Despite the -30¬∞C cold and starvation, they did not lose hope and established this shrine in 1910 as a spiritual sanctuary.
This place, imbued with the indomitable will of the pioneers, remains a power spot that gives great courage to those starting new challenges today.

### 2. üå≤ A Stroll Through the Grounds: Encountering Friends of the Forest

Although not far from the city center, once you enter the shrine grounds, a sense of peace envelops you, as if you've entered a deep forest.

#### **Find the Ezo Risu (Squirrels)!**

The mascot of Obihiro Shrine is undoubtedly the **'Ezo Risu'**. On an early morning walk along the approach, you can often spot adorable squirrels scurrying between the trees. Their appearance, approaching people without fear as if they were messengers of the gods, creates an unforgettable memory. (Please take photos quietly!)

#### **The Haiden (Main Hall) and a Forest Walk**

Offer your prayers in front of the simple yet majestic haiden (main hall) with the 'two bows, two claps, one bow' ritual. After praying, take a walk along the forest path lined with giant oaks and elms, and breathe in plenty of phytoncides.

#### **Hidden Power Spot: Sazare-ishi**

Next to the haiden is the **'Sazare-ishi (Pebble Rock)'**, which is mentioned in the lyrics of the Japanese national anthem. Just as small pebbles gather to become a large boulder, this is a place to pray for **family harmony** and **unity**.

### 4. üçö Nearby Attractions: Sweet Desserts and Butadon

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the tastes of Obihiro.

#### **1. Rokkatei Obihiro Main Store**

- **Location**: 10 minutes by car from the shrine.
- **Features**: This is the main store of Hokkaido's representative dessert brand. The **'Saku Saku Pie'**, available only at the 2nd-floor caf√©, is a must-try! The crispy pie filled with cold cream melts in your mouth. (Free coffee is provided!)

#### **2. Butadon no Pancho**

- **Location**: Right in front of Obihiro Station.
- **Features**: The originator of Obihiro's soul food, **'Butadon (pork bowl)'**. The combination of thick, charcoal-grilled pork infused with a smoky aroma and a sweet-and-savory sauce will have you finishing two bowls of rice in no time.

#### **3. Banei Tokachi (Racetrack)**

- **Location**: 10 minutes by car from the shrine.
- **Features**: The world's only **banei (draft horse) racing**. You can watch thrilling races where huge horses pull heavy sleds over hills.
