### 1. üôè Deities and Origins: The Revolutionaries Who Changed Shinto History

The true value of Yoshida Shrine shines even brighter when you understand its historical background.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deities (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): The Guardian Gods of the Fujiwara Clan„Äë**

This shrine enshrines the **Kasuga-no-kami (four deities)**. They are the same gods as those at Kasuga Taisha in Nara and are the clan deities of the Fujiwara family, who were the most powerful figures during the Heian period.

- **Takemikazuchi & Futsunushi**: Martial gods who pacified the country, they aid in **national peace** and **success**.
- **Ame-no-Koyane**: The god of wisdom and prayers, overseeing **academic achievement** and **career advancement**.
- **Himegami**: A goddess who protects **marital harmony** and **peace**.

#### **„ÄêOrigins and History: The Birth of Yoshida Shinto„Äë**

Yoshida Shrine was founded in 859 to protect the northeastern "demon gate" (kimon) of Heian-kyo (Kyoto). However, it was the Muromachi period priest **'Yoshida Kanetomo'** who made this place the center of Japanese religious history.
He founded **'Yoshida Shinto' (Yuiitsu Shinto)**, which overturned the Buddhist-centric worldview by declaring, "The gods are the foundation of the universe." He then declared that all the gods of the nation were gathered here, making Yoshida Shrine the head shrine of all Shinto. Its influence was so great that at one time, all Shinto priests across the country had to be certified here.

### 2. üå≤ A Stroll Through the Precincts: The Daigengu Where Eight Million Gods Gather

Walking the forest path of Mount Yoshida is healing in itself. Here are some key points you shouldn't miss.

#### **Saijosho Daigengu (ÊñéÂ†¥ÊâÄÂ§ßÂÖÉÂÆÆ)**

The highlight of Yoshida Shrine! This is a unique octagonal building constructed by Yoshida Kanetomo.
Enshrined here are **all 3,132 deities** from every shrine across Japan. This means that praying here is said to have the immense effect of praying at all shrines in Japan! It is usually closed but is opened to the public on special days like New Year's and Setsubun. (Worship from the outside is always possible).

#### **Unique Deities: The Gods of Cooking and Confections**

- **Sankage Shrine (God of Cooking)**: Fujiwara no Yamakage, the founder of Yoshida Shrine, is also known as the originator of the Japanese way of cooking. It's a place visited by chefs and restaurant owners praying for improved skills and prosperity.
- **Kaso Shrine (God of Confections)**: This shrine enshrines Tajimamori, who sought out the tachibana orange (the origin of sweets), and Lin Join, who spread the use of manju (steamed buns). It's a must-visit pilgrimage site for pastry chefs and those who love sweets.

#### **Setsubun Festival and Oni**

Every February, Yoshida Shrine comes alive with the **'Setsubun Festival,'** a ritual to drive out demons (oni). The ceremony, where fearsome demons appear and beans are thrown to ward off evil spirits, is a quintessential winter event in Kyoto.

### 4. üçú Nearby Attractions: Kyoto's Intellect and Cuisine

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the atmosphere of Kyoto's university district.

#### **1. Kyoto University & The Philosopher's Path**

- **Location**: Immediately west of the shrine.
- **Features**: Take a stroll through the campus of Kyoto University, one of Japan's top universities. A short walk east of the shrine leads to the **'Philosopher's Path,'** a perfect place for contemplation. It's beautiful with cherry blossoms in spring and autumn leaves in the fall.

#### **2. Shinpuku Saikan (Black Ramen)**

- **Location**: The branch in front of Kyoto University (10-minute walk).
- **Menu**: You can taste Kyoto's representative **'Black Ramen'** and black fried rice (yakimeshi). It may look salty, but it has a surprisingly light and deep flavor that is exquisite.

#### **3. Heian Jingu Shrine**

- **Access**: 20-minute walk.
- **Features**: Another grand shrine near Yoshida Shrine. Its vast garden and vermilion buildings are beautiful. It's a great place to visit together.
