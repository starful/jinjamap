### 1. üôè Deities and Origins: Warding Off Waves and Guarding the Skies

The deity here was originally a guardian who protected against the ocean waves. However, there's an interesting story of how, as times changed, it came to guard the skies as well.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deity (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Toyoukehime-no-Ookami„Äë**

The main deity, **Toyoukehime**, is the goddess of abundant food, clothing, and shelter.
Originally prayed to for bountiful harvests, today she is known as an all-purpose problem-solver responsible for **business prosperity**, **increased financial luck**, and **household safety**. Among aviation industry workers and flight attendants, she is particularly famous as a reliable guardian deity for 'safe flights'.

#### **„ÄêOrigin and History: The Legend of the Miraculous Torii Gate„Äë**

During the Edo period, this area was reclaimed land. When the dikes collapsed due to rough waves, causing the villagers to suffer, a fox appeared in an old man's dream and said, "If you enshrine me, I will protect (ÂÆà) the hole (Á©¥)." Once a shrine was built, the waves miraculously calmed, and from then on, it was given the name **'Anamori' (protecting the hole)**.

There is also a miracle from modern history. After World War II, when the U.S. military tried to forcibly demolish the shrine for the expansion of Haneda Airport, a series of strange incidents occurred whenever they attempted to remove the massive **'O-torii' (grand gate)**; workers would get injured, or machinery would break down. In the end, the torii was left alone in the airport parking lot for over 50 years before being moved to its current location in 1999, becoming the symbol of the shrine. Thanks to this tenacious vitality, it became known as a place that bestows a strong fortune of 'never collapsing'.

### 2. ‚õ©Ô∏è A Stroll Through the Grounds: Finding Fortune Beyond the Red Tunnel

It's filled with a quiet and mystical atmosphere that's hard to believe is so close to an airport.

#### **Senbon Torii: An Illusion of Red Light**

As you go deeper into the shrine, a tunnel of red torii gates, reminiscent of Fushimi Inari in Kyoto, appears. This path, called **'Senbon Torii'** (a thousand torii gates), was created by people who donated a gate in gratitude for their wishes being granted. Walking under the sunlight filtering through the densely packed red pillars feels like being drawn into a divine world. It's also a perfect spot for stunning photos.

#### **Okunomiya and the Mysterious Sand 'Osuna (Âæ°Á•ûÁ†Ç)'**

At the end of the torii tunnel lies the highlight of this shrine, the **'Okunomiya'** (inner shrine). In a cave beneath its altar, there is an abundance of sacred sand called **'Osuna'**.

- **How to use**: Take some sand in one of the provided envelopes.
  - **At your entrance**: It wards off evil spirits and invites good fortune.
  - **Under your bed**: It helps to heal illnesses and protect your health.
  - **In front of a shop**: It attracts customers and ensures business prosperity.
- **Osuna-kaeshi**: When your wish is granted, it is customary to return double the amount of sand you took, mixed with sand from another place.

#### **Hissho Inari and Shusse Inari**

Within the grounds, there are also shrines for **Hissho Inari**, which brings 'victory,' and **Shusse Inari**, which helps with 'career success.' Those facing promotion exams or important contracts should definitely stop by to receive their positive energy.
