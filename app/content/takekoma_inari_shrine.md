### 1. üôè Deities and Origins: A Land of Plenty Foretold by a White Fox

The majesty of Takekoma Inari Shrine is evident from its history and origins.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deities (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Guardians of Food, Clothing, Shelter, and Industry„Äë**

This shrine enshrines three deities: **Ukanomitama-no-kami (god of food)**, **Ukemochi-no-kami (goddess of food)**, and **Wakumusubi-no-kami (god of life/growth)**.
Together, these three deities govern all aspects that enrich human life, including **bountiful harvests (agriculture)**, **business prosperity (commerce)**, and **industrial development (industry)**. Perhaps for this reason, the shrine constantly attracts a diverse range of people praying for success and stability, from business professionals in suits to farmers.

#### **„ÄêOrigins and History: An Oracle from a Thousand Years Ago„Äë**

The shrine's history dates back to 842, during the Heian period. When Ono no Takamura was inspecting this region, an old man riding a white fox (an incarnation of the Inari deity) appeared and delivered a prophecy: **"If you enshrine me on this Mt. Takekoma, this land will prosper greatly."** In response, the deity was enshrined here from Fushimi Inari in Kyoto, founding Takekoma Inari Shrine.

It later gained the full support of the Date clan, the lords of Sendai, and established itself as the foremost guardian of commerce in the Tohoku region. Having been steadfastly rebuilt through numerous wars and disasters over the centuries, it has become a symbol of indomitable life force and prosperity.

### 2. ü¶ä A Stroll Through the Grounds: A Feast of White Foxes and Red Torii Gates

Upon entering the grounds, you'll find a harmonious blend of magnificent architecture and a mystical atmosphere. Here are the points you shouldn't miss.

#### **Legacy of the Date Clan, the Romon (Ê•ºÈñÄ)**

The grand two-story gate (Romon) standing at the entrance was originally a gate from Iwanuma Castle, which was relocated here. The red lacquer and intricate carvings exude the authority and historical weight of the Date clan.

#### **The Majestic Haiden and Honden**

Rebuilt in the Edo period style, the Haiden (hall of worship) is both splendid and graceful. Stand before the Haiden, bathed in the morning sun, and offer your prayers for business prosperity and family safety with the "two bows, two claps, one bow" ritual. Deep behind it lies the Honden (main hall), where the deity resides.

#### **The Mystical Resting Place of Foxes, Myobusha (ÂëΩÂ©¶Á§æ)**

Next to the Haiden is the **'Myobusha'**, which enshrines the white foxes, the messengers of the deity. The sight of countless stone fox statues lined up is both eerie and sacred. It is said that if you offer fried tofu (aburaage) here while making a wish, the foxes will swiftly deliver your message to the deity.

#### **The Red Tunnel, Senbon Torii and Okumiya**

At the east entrance, you'll find the **'Senbon Torii'**, where red torii gates form a tunnel. Passing through this tunnel of cascading red light leads to the **'Okumiya' (inner shrine)**, the very birthplace of the shrine. Nestled in a quiet forest, the Okumiya is a power spot where the strongest energy is concentrated, so be sure to visit.

### 4. üç£ Nearby Attractions: The Flavors and History of Miyagi

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the tastes and sights of Miyagi Prefecture.

#### **1. Takenoya (A Famous Harakomeshi Restaurant)**

- **Location**: A 20-minute walk from the shrine.
- **Recommended Menu**: This restaurant is considered the originator of **'Harakomeshi' (salmon and salmon roe rice bowl)**, a famous autumn dish of Miyagi Prefecture. This exquisite dish features tender salmon fillets and jewel-like salmon roe generously placed over soy-sauce-flavored rice. (Available only in autumn)

#### **2. Iwanuma Castle Ruins Park**

- **Location**: A 15-minute walk from the shrine.
- **Features**: This is the original site of the Romon gate at the shrine's entrance. Now a park, you can take a leisurely stroll and feel the traces of history. It is also famous for its cherry blossoms in the spring.

#### **3. Sightseeing in Sendai City**

- **Access**: 20 minutes by train.
- **What to see**: Complete your historical journey by visiting Date Masamune's mausoleum, 'Zuihoden,' and the Sendai Castle ruins.
