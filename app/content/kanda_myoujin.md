The powerful energy of Kanda Myoujin comes from the deities enshrined here.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deities (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Victory, Wealth, Prosperity„Äë**

Three deities are enshrined here.

- **Daikokuten (Oonamuchi-no-mikoto)**: The god of abundance and wealth. He helps with **matchmaking** and **business prosperity**.
- **Ebisu (Sukunahikona-no-mikoto)**: The god of fishing and commerce, he oversees **thriving business** and **kaiun (opening up good fortune)**.
- **Masakado (Taira no Masakado)**: A tragic hero and a powerful warrior. With his indomitable spirit that never yields to adversity, he is in charge of **'luck in competitions (kach-iun)'** and **'warding off evil.'**

#### **„ÄêOrigin and History: The Guardian of All Edo„Äë**

Founded in 730, Kanda Myoujin has a deep connection with Tokugawa Ieyasu. Before the pivotal Battle of Sekigahara, a once-in-a-lifetime challenge, Ieyasu prayed for victory here and went on to achieve a miraculous win, unifying the country.
Afterward, Ieyasu designated Kanda Myoujin as the **'Edo Sochinju (guardian of all Edo)'** and moved it to its current location to protect the *kimon* (unlucky direction) of Edo Castle, enshrining it with great reverence. Even today, it is the first place that Tokyo's business people and office workers visit on their first workday of the new year.

### 2. ‚õ©Ô∏è A Stroll Through the Grounds: Ornate Architecture and a Giant Stone Statue

As you enter the shrine grounds, the vibrant vermilion buildings will capture your attention. Here are the points you shouldn't miss.

#### **Zuishinmon Gate and Goshaden**

The two-story gate at the entrance, **'Zuishinmon,'** stands out with its intricate carvings and brilliant colors. Past the gate, the magnificent **'Goshaden (Main Hall)'** appears. It was rebuilt in 1934 with steel-reinforced concrete and is a robust structure that even withstood the Great Tokyo Air Raid.

#### **Japan's Largest Stone Statue of Daikokuten**

On the left side of the grounds stands a giant stone statue of **Daikokuten** with a benevolent smile. At 6.6 meters tall and weighing 30 tons, it is one of the largest stone statues in Japan. Pray for wealth and good relationships in front of it.

#### **Cultural Exchange Hall EDOCCO**

This is a state-of-the-art cultural complex located within the shrine. Unlike the traditional image of a shrine, it features stylish shops and cafes, making it a perfect place to relax after paying respects. Be sure to check out the unique souvenirs sold only here.

### 4. üèôÔ∏è Places to Visit Nearby: A District of Subculture and History

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the diverse attractions of Tokyo.

#### **1. Akihabara Electric Town**

- **Location**: 7-minute walk from the shrine.
- **Features**: The mecca of electronics and subculture, needing no introduction. You can find everything from the latest home appliances to figurines and games. Experiencing a maid cafe will also be an unforgettable memory.

#### **2. Yushima Seido (Confucian Temple)**

- **Location**: 5-minute walk from the shrine (across the Hijiribashi Bridge).
- **Features**: The birthplace of modern education in Japan. It is a tranquil place with the world's largest statue of Confucius, frequently visited by students praying for academic success.

#### **3. Kanda Matsuya (Soba Restaurant)**

- **Location**: 10-minute walk from the shrine.
- **Features**: A long-established soba shop with a 140-year history. A bowl of soba enjoyed in the old building beloved by literary masters will convey the authentic taste of Edo.
