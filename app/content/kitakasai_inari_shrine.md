### 1. üôè Deities and Origins: The Rice Goddess of Ukita

**Kitakasai Inari Shrine (ÂåóËëõË•ø Á®≤Ëç∑Á•ûÁ§æ)** is a sacred site dedicated to **Ukanomitama-no-Mikoto**, the revered Shinto deity of rice, agriculture, and industry. In the Edo period, this area was part of the fertile farming lands of the Ukita region, and the shrine was established by local villagers to ensure a bountiful harvest and the safety of their households.

As is characteristic of Inari shrines, you will find the fox (*Kitsune*) acting as the messenger of the gods throughout the grounds. While the surrounding landscape has transformed from rice paddies into a peaceful residential neighborhood, the shrine remains a vital "power spot" for locals seeking success in business and protection from misfortune.

### 3. üìú Goshuin and Shrine Items: Talismans for Prosperity

While this is a local neighborhood shrine, it is beautifully maintained by the community. During festival seasons or specific monthly dates, visitors seek out *Omamori* (amulets) specifically for business prosperity and household safety. The Fox motif is common on many of the votive items here, symbolizing the keen eye and protection of the Inari deity.
