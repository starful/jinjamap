The master of the Naiku is the supreme deity of Japanese mythology, **Amaterasu-Omikami (the Sun Goddess)**. She is the ancestral deity of the Imperial Family and a being like the light that illuminates the entire world.
Its origin dates back 2,000 years when Yamatohime-no-mikoto received a divine oracle and established the site on the banks of the Isuzu River. This place is the spiritual pillar of Japan and a symbol of peace.

The most distinct feature of Ise Jingu is the **'Shikinen Sengu,'** a ritual where an identical shrine building is constructed on an adjacent plot every 20 years, and the deity is transferred. ### 2. ğŸŒ² A Walk Through the Precincts: Sacred Forest and Clear River

Visiting Ise Jingu is a healing process in itself, just by walking.

#### **A Stroll Through Geku: A Serene Beginning**

Geku, located near Iseshi Station, is calm and solemn. After crossing the 'Hiyokebashi' bridge at the entrance, giant trees welcome you. The **'Goshoden (Main Sanctuary)'** can only be seen from behind a fence, but the sanctity felt beyond it is overwhelming. It is recommended to make personal wishes at the auxiliary shrine, **'Takanomiya'**.

#### **A Stroll Through Naiku: Purification of the Soul**

After taking a bus to Naiku, the **'Ujibashi'** bridge appears, connecting the secular world with the sacred precinct. Clear your mind as you cross this 100-meter-long wooden bridge.
Once across the bridge, you'll find the **'Isuzugawa Mitarashiba'**. This is a place to purify your body and mind by washing your hands in the clear river water. Dipping your hands in the cool water feels as if it clears your head.
Offering a reverent prayer at the bottom of the stairs to the innermost **'Goshoden'** completes the highlight of the pilgrimage.

### 4. ğŸ¡ Nearby Attractions: A Delicious Time-Travel to the Edo Period

After your visit, enjoy a food tour at **'Okage Yokocho'** and **'Oharai Machi'**!

#### **1. Akafuku Main Store (èµ¤ç¦)**

- **Must-Eat**: Ise's signature, **'Akafukumochi'**. The unique shape, with sweet red bean paste covering a rice cake, symbolizes the ripples of the Isuzu River. The taste of the mochi and tea, enjoyed while sitting on the veranda of the main store overlooking the river, is unforgettable.

#### **2. Ise Udon**

- **Features**: Thick, plump noodles are mixed with a dark, rich soy-based sauce. Despite its appearance, it's not salty and has a wonderfully soft texture. Try it at a long-established shop like **'Fukusuke'**.

#### **3. Tekonezushi (Bonito Rice Bowl)**

- **Features**: A fisherman's dish made by placing soy-marinated bonito sashimi over rice. It's an explosion of umami flavor.

#### **4. Meoto Iwa (Wedded Rocks)**

- **Location**: Futami Okitama Shrine (15 minutes by train).
- **Features**: Two rocks standing side by side in the sea, connected by a sacred rope (shimenawa). They symbolize marital harmony and connection.
