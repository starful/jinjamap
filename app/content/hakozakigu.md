### 1. üôè Deities and Origins: The Miraculous Wind that Saved the Nation

The prestige of Hakozakigu Shrine comes from its historical background.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deities (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Hachiman Okami„Äë**

The main deity here is **Emperor Ojin (Hachiman-shin)**. As the god of martial arts and victory, he has been revered by warriors since ancient times. Also enshrined are **Empress Jingu** (Emperor Ojin's mother) and **Tamayorihime**, who oversee safe childbirth and maritime safety, respectively.

#### **„ÄêOrigins and History: Where the Divine Wind Blew„Äë**

The highlight of Hakozakigu Shrine's history is the 'Mongol invasions (Yuan-Goryeo allied forces)' incident during the Kamakura period.
In 1274, when the Mongol army descended upon the seas off Kyushu, the imperial court prayed fervently here for the enemy's repulsion. A massive typhoon then arose, devastating the Mongol fleet. People called this a wind sent by the gods, the **'Kamikaze' (divine wind)**. With this miracle, Hakozakigu Shrine was established as the most powerful shrine protecting the nation.

### 2. üèØ A Stroll Through the Grounds: Feeling the Weight of History

Upon entering the shrine grounds, you are greeted by architectural structures of overwhelming scale.

#### **Romon Gate and the 'Tekkoku Kofuku' Tablet**

The two-story Romon gate, a symbol of the shrine, is a National Important Cultural Property. However, what draws even more attention is the plaque hanging above it: **'Tekkoku Kofuku (ÊïµÂõΩÈôç‰ºè)'**. This doesn't mean "to subjugate the enemy by force," but rather carries the profound meaning of "achieving peace by having the enemy surrender of their own accord through virtue (Âæ≥)." Feel the weight of history standing before these four powerful characters.

#### **Main Hall (Honden) and Worship Hall (Haiden)**

Rebuilt in 1546, the Main Hall and Worship Hall are masterpieces of the grand 'nagare-zukuri' architectural style. Offer a devout prayer here with the 'nirei nihakushu ichirei' (two bows, two claps, one bow) etiquette at this designated Important Cultural Property.

#### **Power Spots: The Gushing Stone and Sacred Pine**

- **Yushuseki (Gushing Stone)**: This is a mysterious stone next to the main hall. It is said that touching it will make your luck "gush forth," so be sure to touch it and receive its energy.
- **Hakomatsu (Sacred Pine)**: A legendary pine tree said to have been planted over the buried placenta of Emperor Ojin. This is the origin of the shrine's name, 'Hakozaki.'

### 4. üçû Places to Visit Nearby: The Romance of Bread and the Sea

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the subtle charms of Hakozaki.

#### **1. Pan Nagata Hakozaki Branch**

- **Location**: 5-minute walk from the shrine.
- **Features**: An essential stop on any Fukuoka bakery pilgrimage! The aroma of freshly baked bread wafts from the quaint wooden building. The specialty **'Mentai France' (Mentaiko Baguette)** is the epitome of crispy on the outside and moist on the inside.

#### **2. Hakozaki Shopping Street**

- **Features**: A shopping street that stretches along the approach to the shrine. It's a great place for a stroll, soaking in the retro atmosphere created by old signboards and modest shops.

#### **3. Shin'en Hana Teien (Flower Garden on the Shrine Approach)**

- **Features**: A Japanese-style garden located within the shrine grounds. It is beautiful with peonies and tree peonies in spring, hydrangeas in summer, and autumn leaves in the fall. If you love flowers, be sure to stop by.
