### 2. ü¶ä A Stroll Through the Grounds: Praying for Great Success with Fried Tofu and Business Cards

The way of worship at Ochobo-san is very unique and lively. Don't go empty-handed; be sure to pick up the necessary items at the entrance!

#### **Worship Essentials: Fried Tofu and a Candle**

It's standard practice to buy a **'bundle of fried tofu (abura-age) and a candle (50 yen)'** from a shop at the shrine entrance.

1. **Offering a Candle**: Place the candle in the candle offering hall (ÌóåÎì±ÏÜå) and light it. This symbolizes the spirit of sacrifice, burning oneself to light up the world, which represents an entrepreneur's dedication.
2. **Offering Fried Tofu**: Place the fried tofu on the altar in front of the main hall. This is an offering to the fox deity, who loves fried tofu, as you make your wish. The massive pile of fried tofu is a truly unique sight here.

#### **The Worship Hall Adorned with Business Cards**

If you look closely in front of the worship hall (haiden), you will see countless business cards tucked into the structure. This is how entrepreneurs appeal to the gods, saying, "Please remember my business!" Be sure to bring a business card to plant your own seeds of success.

#### **The Heavy/Light Stone (Omokaruishi)**

Further inside the grounds, there is an **'Omokaruishi'** stone for divining whether your wish will be granted.

1. Make a wish in front of the stone.
2. Lift the stone.
3. **If it feels lighter than you expected, your wish will come true!** If it feels heavy, it means more effort is required. How much will your wish weigh?
