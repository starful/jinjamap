### 1. üôè Deity and Origin: How a War Hero Became the God of Victory

Behind the grandeur of Togo Shrine lies the story of a hero who defined an era.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deity (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Heihachiro Togo„Äë**

The deity enshrined here is **Heihachiro Togo**.
He was the hero who saved Japan during the Russo-Japanese War in 1905 by miraculously annihilating Russia's Baltic Fleet, then considered the world's strongest, in the Battle of Tsushima. His outstanding leadership and remarkable good fortune transformed him from a mere military man into a deified being who brings victory.
That is why this shrine has become a place to pray for all matters related to 'winning,' such as **victory in sports**, **passing exams**, **success in business**, and **overcoming illness**.

#### **„ÄêOrigin and History: The Legend of the Z Flag„Äë**

On the day of the decisive battle, Admiral Togo hoisted the **'Z Flag'** on his flagship and delivered his famous address: "The fate of the Empire rests on this one battle. Let every man do his utmost."
As the last letter of the alphabet, Z symbolized a point of no return, the resolve for a final, decisive battle. The great victory achieved under this flag astounded the world, and the Z Flag became a symbol of triumph. After his death in 1934, this shrine was built here in Harajuku in response to the ardent wishes of the people.

### 2. üå≤ A Stroll Through the Precincts: The Flag of Victory and a Healing Garden

As you step into the precincts, the noise of Harajuku fades away, replaced by a sacred and solemn atmosphere.

#### **The Path to the Shrine, Lined with Z Flags**

Throughout the shrine grounds, you can see the **'Z Flag,'** a pattern of intersecting yellow, blue, red, and black squares. As you watch the flags flutter, strengthen your own will to win.

#### **The Majestic Main Hall (Honden) and Worship Hall (Haiden)**

Built in the 'Shinmei-zukuri' style, the same as Ise Grand Shrine, the main hall has a clean, unadorned, and dignified feel. Stand before the worship hall, pay your respects with 'two bows, two claps, one bow,' and convey your earnest wishes for the battles ahead in your life. You will surely feel the powerful spirit of Admiral Togo.

#### **Senshintei (The Garden for Cleansing the Heart)**

Next to the main hall is a beautiful Japanese garden called **'Senshintei.'**Èå¶ÈØâ (Nishikigoi) swim in the clear pond, and the seasonal flowers and trees create a picturesque scene. It is the perfect healing spot to clear your mind and find tranquility.

#### **Kaigunkan (Naval Archives)**

This is a small museum displaying Admiral Togo's personal belongings and materials related to the navy of that era. It's recommended for those who wish to see a tangible piece of history.
