#### **ã€Origin and History: The Miracle that Quelled a Rebellionã€‘**

The history of Shinshoji dates back to 940, during the Heian period. When the country was in turmoil due to the rebellion of Taira no Masakado, the high priest Kancho Daisojo, under the emperor's order, brought a statue of Fudo Myoo here and prayed earnestly for 21 days.
On the final day of the prayer, a miracle occurred as the rebellion was suppressed. Following a revelation from Fudo Myoo to "remain in this land and save the people," the temple was established. Its name became **'Shinshoji (æ–°å‹å¯º)'**, meaning **'Newly (æ–°) Victorious (å‹)'**.

The spacious grounds are filled with sights to see. Here are the points you shouldn't miss.

#### **Great Main Hall and the Goma Fire Ritual (è­·æ‘©ç¥ˆç¥·)**

The highlight of Shinshoji! The **'Goma fire ritual'** is held several times a day in the Great Main Hall. It is a ceremony where monks beat drums, chant sutras, and burn wooden sticks (gomagi) in a fire. Try the **'Ohikakari'** experience, where you lightly pass your bag or wallet over the fire. It is said that the power of Fudo Myoo will imbue them and ward off evil spirits. (Anyone can participate, free of charge)

#### **Somon and Niomon Gates**

The massive **Somon Gate** at the entrance and the red **Niomon Gate** boast an overwhelming presence. The giant red lantern (chÅchin) inside the Niomon Gate was donated by merchants from the Tokyo fish market and is a symbol of this place. A must-have photo spot!

#### **Great Pagoda of Peace**

At 58 meters tall, the **'Great Pagoda of Peace'** at the highest point of the grounds is a landmark. On the first floor, you can experience shakyo (sutra copying). It's a great place to calm your mind.

#### **Shusse Inari Shrine**

On the hill to the left of the main hall is the **'Shusse Inari'** shrine. Dedicated to the fox deity, this spot is famous for praying for **'career advancement'** and **'business prosperity'**, just as its name suggests. If you're an office worker dreaming of a promotion, be sure to visit.
