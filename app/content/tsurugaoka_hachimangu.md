#### **ã€Origins and History: The Heart of the Kamakura Shogunateã€‘**

In 1180, the head of the Genji clan, **Minamoto no Yoritomo**, entered Kamakura and began preparations to establish his shogunate. The first thing he did was to move this shrine, which enshrined his ancestral deity, to the center of the city and build it on a grand scale. The city was designed and roads were built with the shrine at its core, so it's no exaggeration to say that Kamakura itself was a city created for Hachimangu.

However, this place is not only a site of glory but also of tragedy. The stone steps in front of the main shrine are where the third shogun, Minamoto no Sanetomo, was assassinated by his own nephew. The history of this place, where the desire for power and its transience coexist, resonates deeply with visitors.
