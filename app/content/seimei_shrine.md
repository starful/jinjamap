The mystique of Seimei Shrine stems from the legendary life of its master, Abe no Seimei.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deity (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Abe no Seimei„Äë**

The enshrined deity here is **Abe no Seimei**. He was a real astronomer and Onmyoji during the Heian period who predicted the nation's fortunes and prevented disasters based on the cutting-edge science of his time, astronomy and the Yin-Yang Five Elements theory.
Thanks to his superhuman abilities, he was deified after his death and is believed to exert powerful influence, especially for **'warding off evil (yakuyoke),'** **'demon dispelling (mayoke),'** and **'victory/success (shoun).'**

#### **„ÄêOrigins and History: Son of a Fox, Became a God„Äë**

There is a mystical birth legend about Seimei that "his mother was a white fox from the forest." Stories of him having the ability to see spirits from a young age and freely commanding shikigami (spirit servants) show what an extraordinary figure he was.
When he passed away in 1005, Emperor Ichijo, to honor his great achievements, had a shrine built on the site of his mansion. A thousand years later, this place remains a spiritual fortress protecting Kyoto.

### 2. ‚≠ê A Stroll Through the Grounds: Into the Pentagram's Barrier

The grounds are small, but filled with traces of the Onmyoji at every turn. Here are the points you shouldn't miss.

#### **Ichino Torii and the Pentagram**

At the shrine's entrance, a golden **'pentagram (star)'** shines in the center of the stone torii. It is very rare to see a star where a shrine's name is usually written. This pentagram is also called the 'Seimei Kikyo' (Seimei Bellflower) and symbolizes the harmony of the five elements and a charm to repel evil.

#### **Ichijo Modoribashi Bridge and Shikigami Statue**

After passing the torii, you'll find a small bridge and a cute(?) demon stone statue next to it. This is a recreation of the legendary bridge, **'Ichijo Modoribashi,'** where Seimei was said to have hidden his shikigami (spirit servants).

#### **Seimei Well (Seimei-i)**

Next to the main hall is a well that Seimei is said to have brought forth with his psychic powers.

- **Features**: It is known as miraculous water that cures illnesses. The spout from which the water flows is turned to face the auspicious direction (eho) of the year every Risshun (the first day of spring). It's very popular as a phone wallpaper, with rumors that it brings good luck.

#### **Warding Peach (Yakuyoke-momo)**

In front of the main hall stands a shiny peach statue.

- **Experience**: Rub the peach to transfer your uneasy feelings or bad energy to it. The peach has been considered a fruit that wards off evil since ancient times. You will feel much lighter in spirit.

### 4. üç∞ Places to Visit Nearby: Kyoto's History and Sweetness

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the charming spots nearby.

#### **1. Nijo Castle**

- **Getting there**: 10 minutes by bus.
- **Features**: This was Tokugawa Ieyasu's stronghold in Kyoto. The Ninomaru Palace (a National Treasure) and the "nightingale floors" (corridors that chirp when walked on) are must-sees.

#### **2. Kyoto Gyoen (Kyoto Imperial Palace)**

- **Getting there**: 15-minute walk.
- **Features**: The former imperial palace and its surrounding park where the emperor once lived. The wide gravel paths and woods are perfect for a stroll.

#### **3. Cafe Rhinebeck (Pancakes)**

- **Location**: 5-minute walk from the shrine.
- **Menu**: One of Kyoto's best **pancake** spots. Their thick, fluffy classic pancakes melt in your mouth. Perfect for a sugar boost after your shrine visit.
