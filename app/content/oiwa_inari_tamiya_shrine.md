**Oiwa Inari Tamiya Shrine (ÊñºÂ≤©Á®≤Ëç∑Áî∞ÂÆÆÁ•ûÁ§æ)** in Shinkawa is a site where Japanese urban legend meets historical reality. For many, the name "Oiwa" immediately conjures images of the vengeful ghost from the famous Kabuki play, *Tokaido Yotsuya Kaidan*. However, the truth preserved at this shrine tells a much more inspiring story.

**The Real Oiwa-sama**
The shrine is dedicated to **Oiwa-no-Mikoto**, a real woman who lived in the early Edo period (died 1636). Contrary to the tragic figure portrayed on stage, the historical Oiwa was a woman of exceptional virtue. She and her husband, Tamiya Iemon, were known for their tireless work and devotion to the Inari deity, which led to the miraculous revival of the Tamiya family's fortunes. Neighbors began to worship her small home shrine, calling it "Oiwa Inari," believing that following her example of faith would bring prosperity to their own households.

**The Shinkawa Connection**
While the original shrine is in Yotsuya, this Shinkawa branch has a deep-rooted history. During the late Edo period, the Tamiya family maintained a secondary residence in Shinkawa due to its proximity to the Shogunate‚Äôs strategic warehouses. In 1879 (Meiji 12), after a fire destroyed the Yotsuya site and following the immense popularity of Kabuki plays featuring Oiwa, the shrine was officially relocated to Shinkawa to accommodate the many actors and fans from the nearby entertainment districts of Nihonbashi and Ningyocho. Today, it remains a "mecca" for Kabuki actors who visit to pay their respects before performing any play related to Oiwa.

### 3. üìú Goshuin and Shrine Items: Talismans for Success and Safety

The **Goshuin** (holy stamp) of Oiwa Inari Tamiya Shrine is highly prized, especially among those in the performing arts. It features bold calligraphy and the distinct stamp of the Tamiya family. 

**Blessings for Performers and Families**
The shrine offers unique **Omamori** (amulets) focused on:
*   **Geinou Joutatsu (Success in Performing Arts):** Given the link to Kabuki, actors and singers visit to pray for the success of their shows.
*   **Kanai Anzen (Household Safety):** Honoring the historical Oiwa‚Äôs dedication to her family‚Äôs prosperity.
*   **Yakuyoke (Warding off Evil):** Many visit to ensure that the "scary" version of the legend stays purely on the stage, seeking protection from real-world misfortune.

### 4. üåÉ What to See Nearby: The Spirit of Old Shinkawa

Shinkawa was once the heart of the Edo period's wholesale district, especially for sake and soy sauce.
*   **Sumida River Terrace:** Just a 3-minute walk away, the riverside terrace offers a beautiful view of the **Eitai Bridge**. It is a perfect spot for a calm walk after your visit.
*   **The Sake Warehouse Markers:** Look around the Shinkawa area for historic plaques that mark where the great sake warehouses of the Shogunate once stood.
*   **Hatchobori:** The neighboring district, once home to the Edo police force (*Yoriki*), is full of charming small eateries and bars.
