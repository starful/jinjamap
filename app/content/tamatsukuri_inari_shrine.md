It is also a romantic sanctuary inhabited by 'foxes' that are said to mend broken bonds and help new love blossom.

Are you tired of the typical Osaka tourist spots? If so, join me on a special journey through time to Tamatsukuri Inari Shrine, where the breath of history and the mystery of myth coexist.

#### **ã€Origin and History: From the Sweat of Artisans to the Guardian of Osaka Castleã€‘**

The history of this site dates back to 12 B.C., during the reign of the 11th Emperor Suinin. At that time, a group of artisans called the **'Tamatsukuribe (ç‰é€ éƒ¨)'**, who crafted sacred beads (magatama) for the imperial court, lived in this area. The shrine originated as a place where they enshrined their ancestral gods, praying for the refinement of their skills and the well-being of their clan. The history of the Curved Jewel (magatama), one of the Three Sacred Treasures of Japan, breathes within these grounds.

As time passed into the Warring States period, **Toyotomi Hideyoshi**, who unified the nation, designated this shrine as the guardian (chinjusha) of Osaka Castle upon its construction. This was due to its crucial location, which, according to Feng Shui, protected the castle from the kimon (unlucky direction). Hideyoshi's mother, O-mandokoro, and his wife, Yodo-dono, were deeply devoted to this shrine. In particular, when her son Hideyori was born, Yodo-dono buried his **umbilical cord and placenta (ena, èƒè¡£)** here, praying with tears that her son would become the master of Osaka Castle and live a long, healthy life.

Although the Toyotomi clan perished and the shrine was burned down in the Siege of Osaka, it was rebuilt through the faith of the people and continues its profound history to this day.

The grounds are not large, but they are filled with gem-like spots that will make you pause.

#### **The Couple Fox Statues (å¤«å©¦ã‚­ãƒ„ãƒåƒ): A Token of Love**

Pay attention to the pair of fox statues guarding the front of the worship hall. Unlike the usual solemn fox statues, the foxes here look very affectionate. The mother fox on the left holds a lovely cub in her arms, while the father fox on the right holds a sacred jewel in his mouth, and they gaze at each other. This **'couple fox'** is said to grant wishes for marital harmony, family happiness, and for those seeking a good partner, making it a famous spot for romantic fulfillment. It's not uncommon to see couples taking pictures with their cheeks pressed together.

#### **Hoezuka Daimyojin (èƒè¡£å¡šå¤§æ˜ç¥): Weaving the Threads of Fate**

This is the spot on the grounds where the most powerful energy can be felt. It is the very place where Toyotomi Hideyori's umbilical cord is buried.
The umbilical cord, which connects a mother and child, symbolizes the strongest 'thread of connection.' Therefore, this place is not only for **couples wishing for children** or praying for a **safe childbirth**, but it is also known as a supreme power spot for mending broken relationships or firmly tying new ones (enmusubi). Standing before the stone mound beyond the torii gate, quietly envision the connections in your heart.

#### **Naniwa-Tamatsukuri Museum**

In a corner of the shrine, a small museum displays ancient magatama and pottery excavated from the area. It is a precious space where you can feel the breath of the artisans who toiled to carve beads here 2,000 years ago. (Free admission, opens upon request)
