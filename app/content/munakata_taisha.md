The mystique of Munakata Taisha begins with the story of three goddesses who protect the sea.

This shrine enshrines the three daughters of Amaterasu Omikami (the sun goddess): **Tagorihime**, **Tagitsuhime**, and **Ichikishimahime**.

- **Okinoshima (Okitsumiya)**: The solitary island 60km from the mainland, where the eldest daughter, Tagorihime, resides.
- **Oshima (Nakatsumiya)**: The middle island, where the second daughter, Tagitsuhime, resides.
- **Tajima (Hetsumiya)**: The main shrine on the mainland, where the youngest daughter, Ichikishimahime, resides, and which we can visit.

These three goddesses command the sea's mist and waves, safeguarding maritime and traffic safety. They are also revered as **'Michihiraki (path-opening)'** deities who guide people onto the right path in the long journey of life.

#### **ã€Origins and History: Okinoshima, the Island Where Gods Dwellã€‘**

The history of Munakata Taisha is the history of ancient exchange between the Korean peninsula and the continent itself. From the 4th to the 9th century, countless treasures were offered on Okinoshima to pray for safe voyages, which have remained intact to become the **'80,000 national treasures.'**
To this day, Okinoshima remains a forbidden sanctuary where women are not allowed, and even men can only enter after undergoing a strict purification ritual. In recognition of this sanctity and historical value, it was registered as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2017.

### 2. ğŸŒ² Strolling the Grounds: Encountering National Treasures and a Sacred Forest

The place accessible to the general public for worship is the **'Hetsumiya'** on the mainland. Even here, you can fully experience the mystique of Okinoshima.

#### **Honden and Haiden (Main and Worship Halls)**

The Honden (main hall), a designated National Important Cultural Property, is an elegant structure reconstructed in 1578. Offer a reverent prayer before the wooden architecture, with its gracefully curved roof and traces of time.

#### **Takamiya Saijo (Ancient Ritual Site)**

Follow the forest path behind the main hall to find the **'Takamiya Saijo,'** the most sacred place and the origin of Munakata Taisha. It is an ancient ritual site composed only of trees and stones, without any buildings. Standing here, you can feel a thrill as if the mystical energy of ancient times envelops your entire body. (A highly recommended power spot!)

#### **Shinpokan (Treasure Museum)**

This is where the **80,000 national treasures** excavated from Okinoshima are stored and exhibited. Artifacts that showcase the essence of ancient East Asian exchange, such as golden rings, Persian glass bowls, and bronze mirrors, are breathtaking to behold. It's an essential stop that you must visit after praying at the shrine.

#### **Daini-gu & Daisan-gu (Second & Third Shrines)**

Next to the main hall are the Daini-gu and Daisan-gu, which enshrine the two older sister goddesses (Tagorihime and Tagitsuhime) from the islands. By praying here, it is as if you are meeting all three goddesses without having to cross the sea to the islands. These buildings are especially significant as they were relocated from the former main sanctuary of Ise Grand Shrine.

### 4. ğŸŒŠ Places to Visit Nearby: The Path of Light and Fresh Sashimi

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the beautiful surrounding scenery and delicious food.

#### **1. Miyajidake Shrine**

- **Access**: 20 minutes by car.
- **Features**: Famous for the **'Path of Light,'** where the setting sun aligns perfectly with the straight path leading to the sea (limited to February and October). Japan's largest sacred rope (shimenawa) is also a must-see.

#### **2. Michi-no-Eki Munakata**

- **Access**: 15 minutes by car.
- **Features**: One of Kyushu's largest roadside stations. It's filled with fresh seafood raised in the rough waves of the Genkai Sea. The **'squid sashimi'** and **'seafood rice bowl (kaisen-don)'** are so famous that people line up for them.

#### **3. Oshima (Nakatsumiya)**

- **Access**: 25 minutes by ferry from Konominato Port.
- **Features**: If you have time, take a ferry to Oshima, where the second goddess resides. On a clear day, you can see Okinoshima in the distance from the Yohaisho (place of worship from afar).
