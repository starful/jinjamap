Kuzuryu Shrine Shingu is located right next to the Hakone Shrine grounds. Just walking along the magnificent approach to Hakone Shrine is enough to begin the purification of your heart.

#### **Worshipping Alongside Hakone Shrine**

Pass through the giant **'Heiwa no Torii' (Peace Torii)** standing tall by the shore of Lake Ashi and walk along the path lined with cedar trees that seem to pierce the sky. After climbing the 89 stone steps and paying respects at the main hall of Hakone Shrine, turn your head to the right, and the Kuzuryu Shrine Shingu, small yet radiating a powerful aura, will reveal itself.
The shrine building is modest compared to Hakone Shrine, but the combination of red lacquer and a serene atmosphere fosters a deeper connection with the deity. Here, recall the dragon god's thousand-year-old oath and offer a heartfelt prayer.

There is one place at Kuzuryu Shrine Shingu that you must absolutely not miss. It is the **'Ryujinsui (é¾ç¥æ°´)'** located in front of the worship hall.
Clear, cold water gushes endlessly from the mouths of a nine-headed dragon sculpture. This water is natural spring water from deep within Mt. Hakone and is said to be filled with the dragon god's energy.

It is said that if you put it in a special bottle sold at the shrine office and take it home to drink or use in cooking, good energy will spread throughout your home.

#### **2. Amazake Chaya (ç”˜é…’èŒ¶å±‹)**

- **Why it's recommended**: A traditional teahouse that has served as a resting place for travelers for 400 years since the Edo period. - **How to get there**: Take the Hakone Tozan Bus (K/H lines) and get off at the 'Amazake-chaya' stop.
