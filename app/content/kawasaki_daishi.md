The prestige of Kawasaki Daishi begins with a dramatic legend from a thousand years ago.

#### **ã€Enshrined Deity (Gohonzon): Yakuyoke Kobo Daishiã€‘**

The main deity here is **'Yakuyoke (Evil-Warding) Kobo Daishi.'** 'Kobo Daishi' is an honorific title for the monk **Kukai**, the founder of the Shingon sect of Japanese Buddhism.
He is revered as a living Buddha and is especially famous for his miraculous power to **dispel evil fortune**, **prevent disasters**, and **grant wishes**. This is why people facing critical years in their lives (yakudoshi) flock here from all over the country.

#### **ã€Origin and History: A Samurai's Dream and a Fisherman's Netã€‘**

In 1128, a samurai named Hirama Kanenori, who was living as a fisherman after being unjustly exiled, received a revelation from a high priest in a dream. The next day, when he cast his net into the sea, he pulled up a brilliantly shining **wooden statue of Kobo Daishi**.
After he enshrined the statue with great care, a miracle occurred: his false accusations were cleared, and he was reinstated to his samurai status. A high priest named Sonken, hearing of this, founded a temple. This was the beginning of 'Hirama's Temple,' Kawasaki Daishi (Heiken-ji). It is truly a land of miracles where hope was salvaged from despair.

### 2. ğŸ¯ A Stroll Through the Grounds: The Overwhelming Four Heavenly Kings and the Red Pagoda

The temple grounds are a space where solemnity and tranquility coexist. Here are the must-see points.

#### **Daisanmon (Main Gate) and the Shitenno**

The massive gate at the entrance, the **'Daisanmon,'** is overwhelming. On either side of the gate stand colossal statues of the Shitenno (Four Heavenly Kings), with glaring eyes to ward off evil spirits. Just passing through this gate feels as if a weight has been lifted from your mind.

#### **Dai-Hondo (Main Hall) and the Ogoma (Homa) Ritual**

In the Main Hall, the **'Ogoma (Homa)'** ritual is performed daily. It is a solemn ceremony where monks light a sacred fire and chant sutras to burn away worldly desires and evil fortune. Experience the powerful energy created by the combination of drum beats and blazing flames.

#### **Hakkaku Gojunoto (Octagonal Five-Storied Pagoda)**

Next to the Main Hall stands the magnificent **'Octagonal Five-Storied Pagoda,'** adorned in red and gold. This octagonal, five-storied pagoda is a rare and unique architectural style in Japan, making it the best spot for photos.

#### **The Healing Nade-Yakushi**

In front of the Yakushi-den Hall, there is a statue called **'Nade-Yakushi.'** It is believed that if you rub the same part of the statue as your ailment, it will be healed. As a result, the statue's knees and shoulders are always polished smooth. Rub it while praying for good health.

### 4. ğŸ¡ Nearby Attractions: A Street of Rhythmic Flavors

The charm of visiting a temple extends to the shopping street (Nakamise-dori) right in front of it.

#### **1. Tontoko Ame (The Sound of Cutting Candy)**

- **Features**: As you enter the shopping street, you'll hear the cheerful "ton, toko, ton, toko" sound from cutting boards. This is the sound of the famous **'Sekidome Ame (cough-drop candy)'** being cut. Sample the soft, sweet candy and watch the artisans' rhythmic knife work.

#### **2. Kuzumochi (Sumiyoshi)**

- **Features**: The signature snack of Kawasaki Daishi! **'Kuzumochi'** is a dessert of chewy rice cakes generously topped with kinako (roasted soybean flour) and kuromitsu (black sugar syrup). The sweetness enjoyed at the long-established 'Sumiyoshi' shop will make you forget all your fatigue from the visit.

#### **3. Daishi Park (Chinese Garden)**

- **Location**: 10-minute walk.
- **Features**: Inside the large park next to the temple is a beautiful Chinese-style garden called **'Shenxiu-en.'** It's a great place to enjoy a stroll in an exotic atmosphere.
