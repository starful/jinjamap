### 1. üôè Deities and Origins: Mercy Blooming Where Pure Water Springs

Behind the grandeur of Kiyomizu-dera lies the story of a monk's mystical dream and a general's change of heart.

#### **„ÄêPrincipal Deity (Âæ°Êú¨Â∞ä): Eleven-headed Thousand-armed Kannon Bodhisattva„Äë**

The principal deity here is the **Eleven-headed Thousand-armed Kannon Bodhisattva**. She is the embodiment of mercy, said to see and relieve all the suffering of living beings with her eleven faces and a thousand hands. It is believed to possess powerful abilities to grant all worldly wishes, including **health**, **academic success**, **career advancement**, and **good relationships**. As a *hibutsu* (Áßò‰ªè), a hidden Buddha revealed only once every 33 years, it is all the more mysterious.

#### **„ÄêOrigin and History: The Deer Hunter and the Monk„Äë**

In 778, the ascetic monk Enchin followed a revelation in a dream and arrived at the pure waterfall on Mount Otowa. There, he received a sacred log from a hermit he met and carved a statue of Kannon.
Two years later, the general Sakanoue no Tamuramaro, who came to hunt deer, met Enchin, repented for the sin of taking a life, and built the temple to pray for his wife's safe childbirth. This was the beginning of Kiyomizu-dera. It was named **'Kiyomizu-dera (Pure Water Temple)'** after the waterfall.

### 2. üèØ A Stroll Through the Grounds: The Stage in the Sky and the Three Streams

The temple grounds are built three-dimensionally along the mountainside, making it fun to walk around. Here are the key points you shouldn't miss.

#### **Ni≈çmon (Deva Gate) and the Three-Story Pagoda**

The red **'Ni≈çmon'** at the entrance is the face of Kiyomizu-dera. Behind it, the magnificent 31-meter-high **'Three-Story Pagoda'** is a landmark visible from anywhere in Kyoto. A photo here is a must!

#### **The Stage of Kiyomizu (Main Hall)**

The highlight of the temple! This is the **'Stage of Kiyomizu,'** where 139 massive Zelkova pillars, interlocked without a single nail, support the main hall. The view of Kyoto city and the seasonal landscapes from this stage, seemingly floating 13 meters (the height of a 4-story building) in the air, is breathtaking.

#### **Jishu Shrine (Sanctuary of Love)**

Right behind the main hall is **'Jishu Shrine,'** dedicated to the god of love. The **'Love Stones'** are a legendary popular spot; it's said that if you can walk from one stone to the other with your eyes closed, your love will be fulfilled. (It is currently under renovation, but its spiritual energy remains!)

#### **Otowa-no-taki (Otowa Waterfall)**

Heading down from the main hall, you'll find the **'Otowa Waterfall,'** where three streams of water fall. You use a long ladle to catch and drink the water while making a wish. The streams are said to represent **'academic success,' 'success in love,'** and **'health and longevity,'** respectively. But be careful‚Äîthey say if you greedily drink from all three, the benefits will disappear, so choose just one!

After visiting the temple, take a walk through the old streets of Kyoto.

- **Location**: The sloping path in front of Kiyomizu-dera's main gate.
- **Features**: A stone-paved slope with a fearsome(?) legend that "if you fall, you will die within three years." The street, lined with traditional houses and souvenir shops, is the epitome of Kyoto's atmosphere.

#### **2. % Arabica Kyoto (% Coffee)**
