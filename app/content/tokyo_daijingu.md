### 2. üíñ A Stroll Through the Grounds: Finding Hearts Full of Excitement

The shrine grounds are not large, but they are filled with a charming and lovely atmosphere. Here are some key points you shouldn't miss.

#### **Finding Hidden Hearts (Inome Pattern)**

Look closely at the metal ornaments and wood carvings on the shrine buildings. You'll find heart-shaped patterns hidden in various spots. These are actually a traditional pattern called **'Inome (Áå™„ÅÆÁõÆ),'** which means 'boar's eye,' and is meant to ward off evil spirits. Here, however, it's known as a **'heart that grants love.'** There's a popular belief that finding these hidden hearts, taking a picture, and setting it as your phone's wallpaper will boost your luck in love, so be sure to give it a try!

#### **The Sacred Tree and Pure Water**

Take a deep breath under the giant sacred tree (Á•ûÊú®, shinboku) next to the main hall and feel its pure energy. Don't forget to purify your body and mind with the water flowing from the dragon sculpture at the temizuya (hand-washing pavilion) near the entrance.

#### **Iitomi Inari Shrine**

On one side of the grounds, you'll find the 'Iitomi Inari Shrine' with its impressive red torii gates. It enshrines the god of food, clothing, shelter, and business prosperity. It is said to be particularly effective for those dreaming of a career in the entertainment industry, attracting many aspiring celebrities.

### 4. üçΩÔ∏è Nearby Attractions: The Romance of Kagurazaka

After visiting the shrine, take a stroll through Kagurazaka, 'Tokyo's Little Paris,' which is within walking distance.

#### **1. Kagurazaka Street**

- **On Foot**: 10 minutes.
- **Features**: A charming street where stylish cafes, traditional restaurants (ryotei), and French eateries coexist along narrow alleyways (yokocho). It's also a famous filming location for dramas and becomes a pedestrian-only street on weekends, making it perfect for a walk.

#### **2. Canal Cafe**

- **Location**: Right next to Iidabashi Station.
- **Features**: A waterside cafe and restaurant located on the canal. It's the perfect place to enjoy a relaxing cup of coffee on the terrace while gazing at the water on a sunny day. The view during cherry blossom season is breathtaking.

#### **3. Koishikawa Korakuen Gardens**

- **Location**: 15-minute walk.
- **Features**: A beautiful traditional Japanese garden located next to the Tokyo Dome. It's the best place to heal and experience tranquil nature in the middle of the city.
