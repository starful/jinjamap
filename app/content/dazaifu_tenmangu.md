The deity enshrined here is **Sugawara no Michizane (ËèÖÂéüÈÅìÁúü)**, a leading scholar and politician of the Heian period.
Known as a child prodigy, his exceptional intellect elevated him to the high position of Minister of the Right. However, he was unjustly demoted to Dazaifu due to the slander of his political rivals. After he passed away in lonely exile, a series of natural disasters struck Kyoto. People believed these were caused by his vengeful spirit and began to worship him as **'Tenjin (Â§©Á•û, a heavenly deity)'** to appease him.

Over time, the once-feared Tenjin-sama came to be revered in honor of his lifetime achievements as the **'God of Learning,'** the **'God of Sincerity,'** and the **'God of Culture and Arts.'** Today, as the head of all 12,000 Tenmangu shrines across Japan, this place is filled with the unceasing prayers of worshippers wishing for exam success, academic achievement, and career success.

#### **„ÄêOrigins and History: The Legend Born Where the Ox Stood Still„Äë**

In the year 903, after Michizane passed away, the ox pulling the cart carrying his remains suddenly stopped and refused to move. People took this as a sign of Michizane's will and interred his remains on that very spot. Dazaifu Tenmangu was built over his grave. This means the shrine is **both a sacred site and a tomb**.

In front of the main hall stands the legendary plum tree **'Tobiume (È£õÊ¢Ö)'**. The story goes that when Michizane was leaving Kyoto, he composed a poem lamenting his departure from the tree. Understanding his heart, the tree flew overnight to be with him here. For over 1,000 years, it has remained steadfast, always being the first to blossom each year, showing its loyalty to its master.

### 2. üå≤ Strolling the Grounds: Crossing the Past, Present, and Future

The path to worship at Dazaifu Tenmangu is not just a simple road. It is a special course imbued with meanings of purification and enlightenment.

#### **The Three Bridges: Past, Present, and Future**

Over the 'Shinji-ike' (a pond shaped like the character for 'heart' or 'mind') at the entrance to the grounds, there are three red bridges.

1.  **Taiko-bashi (arched bridge)**: Symbolizes the past. You should cross it without looking back.
2.  **Hira-bashi (flat bridge)**: Symbolizes the present.
3.  **Taiko-bashi (arched bridge)**: Symbolizes the future. You should cross carefully to avoid stumbling.
    By crossing these bridges, you cleanse your mind and body as you enter the sacred realm of the deity.

#### **Goshinkyu (The Sacred Ox)**

Don't miss the ox statues, known as **'Goshinkyu,'** placed throughout the grounds. They commemorate the ox that carried Michizane's remains. There's a belief that **"if you touch a part of your body that is ailng and then touch the same part on the ox, you will be healed,"** and **"rubbing the ox's head will grant you wisdom."** Because of this, the ox's head and horns are always polished to a shine.

#### **The Main Hall (Honden) and Tobiume**

The Main Hall, a designated Important Cultural Property, is a masterpiece of the flamboyant Momoyama architectural style. To the right of the main hall stands the legendary plum tree, **'Tobiume.'** The shrine is at its most beautiful from early February to mid-March when its 6,000 plum trees are in full bloom.

### 4. üç° What to See Nearby: Dazaifu's Tastes and Sights

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the charms of Dazaifu with all your senses.

#### **1. Umegae Mochi: The Specialty of the Sando (Approach Path)**

The path leading from Dazaifu Station to the shrine is lined with shops. The one thing you must try here is **'Umegae Mochi' (plum branch rice cake)**. It's a grilled rice cake filled with sweet red bean paste, crispy on the outside and chewy on the inside. Legend has it that eating this cake wards off illness and clears the mind.

#### **2. Starbucks Dazaifu Tenmangu Omotesando Store**

This Starbucks features a unique design by architect Kengo Kuma. Its interior, which resembles a lattice of interwoven wooden sticks created using traditional joinery techniques, is a work of art in itself. It is also a famous spot for photo ops.

#### **3. Kyushu National Museum**

Located right behind the shrine, this massive glass building houses a wide range of artifacts under the theme of 'Cultural Exchange with Asia.' It is highly recommended for those who want a deeper understanding of history and culture.
