### 2. â›°ï¸ A Stroll Through the Grounds: Facing the Sacred Mountain

From the moment you step onto the grounds, you can feel a change in the air. Take a walk along the path filled with a sacred atmosphere and lush forests.

#### **Haiden and Mitsutorii**

Passing through the giant torii gate and walking up the approach, the magnificent Haiden appears. Here, you bow twice, clap twice, and bow once towards Mount Miwa. Behind the Haiden, in a forbidden area (Kinsokuchi), lies the mystical **'Mitsutorii'**, a sacred gate that can only be seen during special rituals or by special application.

#### **The Cure-all Spring, Sai Shrine (ç‹­äº•ç¥ç¤¾)**

To the left of the Haiden, along the 'Path of Medicine' (Kusuri-michi), you'll find **'Sai Shrine'**, which enshrines a god of healing. Here, medicinal water called **'Goshinsui (ã”ç¥æ°´, sacred water)'** springs forth.
Thanks to the legend that "drinking this water cures any illness," it is bustling with people from all over the country who come to collect it. Purify your body and mind with a cup of this clear, cold water. (Sterilized cups are provided.)

#### **The Stroking Rabbit (Nade-usagi)**

In front of the Sanshuden (assembly hall), there is a cute rabbit statue. It is said that by stroking this rabbit, created due to a mythological connection, the afflicted part of your own body will be healed and your luck will improve. Make a wish as you stroke the rabbit's well-polished head.

#### **Climbing Mount Miwa (Sanshin)**

At Sai Shrine, you can obtain permission to climb Mount Miwa, the sacred mountain body itself. (Round trip: 2-3 hours)
However, this is not just a hike; it is a form of **'worshipful climbing (Tohai)'**. Photography and eating/drinking are prohibited, and one must ascend in a state of meditative silence, fully absorbing the sacred energy. The thrill you feel at the rock altar (Okutsu-iwakura) at the summit will be an unforgettable experience.
