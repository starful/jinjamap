### 1. üôè Deities and Origins: A Miracle Built from a Woman's Prayer

Behind the splendor of Yutoku Inari Shrine lies the deep faith of one woman.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deities (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Guardians of Food, Clothing, and Shelter„Äë**

This shrine enshrines three deities: **Ukanomitama-no-Okami (god of grains)**, **Omiyanome-no-Okami (goddess of arts)**, and **Sarutahiko-no-Okami (god of guidance)**.
As they are gods who enrich our lives, help talents blossom, and illuminate our paths, they bestow blessings across all aspects of life, including **business prosperity**, **family safety**, **traffic safety**, and **improvement in artistic skills**.

#### **„ÄêOrigin and History: The Legacy of Princess Manko„Äë**

In 1687, during the early Edo period, **Princess Manko**, the wife of the lord of the Hizen Kashima domain, brought a divided spirit of the Inari deity enshrined in the Kyoto Imperial Palace when she married into the family.
She spent her life praying here for the well-being of the people and vowed to become a deity to help them even after her death. The shrine's name was derived from her posthumous title, 'Yutoku-in (Á•êÂæ≥Èô¢)'. The unique architectural style, built on a cliff, is reminiscent of Kiyomizu-dera in Kyoto.

### 2. ‚õ©Ô∏è A Stroll Through the Precincts: The Red Path to the Sky

The shrine grounds are three-dimensionally arranged along the mountainside, making it a joy to walk through.

#### **Main Hall on the Cliff (Kakezukuri)**

The **'Kakezukuri'** style, where tall pillars support the main hall, is the symbol of Yutoku Inari Shrine. The combination of red lacquer and ornate decorations boasts a dazzling beauty. (An elevator is available, so anyone can easily access it!)

#### **Red Torii Tunnel and Okunoin**

A mountain path next to the main hall is lined with an endless series of red torii gates. Following this mystical tunnel to the mountain's summit leads to the **'Okunoin (Inner Shrine)'**. The panoramic view of the Ariake Sea and Kashima City from here is incredibly refreshing. It's a moment where you feel the climb was truly worthwhile.

#### **Iwasaki Shrine (Matchmaking)**

By the pond below the main hall is the Iwasaki Shrine, famous for **'matchmaking (enmusubi)'**. Write your wish for love on a heart-shaped ema (votive plaque).

### 4. üç∂ Nearby Attractions: A Sake Town and Hot Springs

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the tastes and sights of Saga.

#### **1. Hizen Hama Shuku (Sake Brewery Street)**

- **Access**: 10 minutes by car.
- **Features**: A street lined with breweries dating back to the Edo period. You can taste and purchase famous sake like 'Nabeshima.' The street, with its old-world charm, is also perfect for a stroll.

#### **2. Ureshino Onsen**

- **Access**: 30 minutes by car.
- **Features**: Famous as one of Japan's top three **'hot springs for beautiful skin.'** Soaking in the smooth, silky water will leave your skin feeling soft. Don't miss the famous **'onsen yudofu'** (hot spring tofu).

#### **3. Monzen Shopping Street**

- **Location**: Right in front of the shrine.
- **Food**: The **'Inari Yokan,'** sold in the shopping arcade at the entrance to the shrine path, is fun to eat by popping it open.
