### ğŸš¶ Nearby Attractions

Hiyoshi Taisha's location in Sakamoto makes it a perfect base for exploring one of Japan's most historically rich regions.

1.  **Enryaku-ji Temple (å»¶æš¦å¯º):** A visit to Hiyoshi Taisha is incomplete without a trip up Mount Hiei to its spiritual partner, Enryaku-ji. 2.  **Sakamoto Town (å‚æœ¬):** The town at the foot of the mountain is a destination in itself. Sakamoto flourished as the home of retired monks from Enryaku-ji, and their former residences, known as *satobÅ*, feature magnificent gardens that are open to the public. The town is also famous for its impressive stone walls, built by a guild of expert masons known as the *AnÅ-shÅ«*. 3.  **Lake Biwa (çµç¶æ¹–):** Japan's largest freshwater lake offers a wealth of recreational and scenic opportunities.
