The origins of Shiogama Shrine are intertwined with the adventures of mythological heroes.

#### **„ÄêEnshrined Deities (Âæ°Á•≠Á•û): Guides Who Open the Way„Äë**

Three deities are enshrined here.

- **Betsugu (Shiotsuchi-oji-no-kami)**: The main deity of this shrine, known as the **'God of Salt'** and the **'God of Guidance.'** He brought prosperity by teaching people salt-making techniques and is revered as a wise god who guides paths across the sea and through life. He grants blessings for **safe childbirth**, **traffic safety**, and **good fortune**.
- **Left and Right Palaces (Takemikazuchi & Futsunushi)**: Two warrior gods who pacified the country. They govern **luck in competitions**, **success**, and **warding off evil**.

#### **„ÄêOrigins and History: A 1200-Year Guardian„Äë**

According to myth, Shiotsuchi-oji-no-kami acted as a guide when the two warrior gods went to pacify the east (Tohoku). After completing their mission, the warrior gods returned, but the old god remained here and taught the people how to make salt by boiling seawater. The shrine 'Shiogama' (salt cauldron) was built to honor his grace.
During the Edo period, it received full support from the Date Masamune clan, attaining its current splendid and grand appearance. The main hall, designated as a National Important Cultural Property, is a masterpiece showcasing the finest craftsmanship of its time.

### 2. üèØ A Stroll Through the Grounds: 202 Steps and Mystical Cherry Blossoms

The path into the shrine grounds is a journey of discipline and healing in itself. Here are the must-see spots.

#### **Otoko-zaka (202 Stone Steps)**

These are the **202 steep stone steps** leading to the shrine's main gate. Although they will leave you breathless, it is said that one piece of misfortune falls away with each step you climb. The feeling of exhilaration upon passing through the **'Zuishinmon Gate'** at the top is indescribable. (For those who find it difficult, you can use the gentler 'Onna-zaka' slope!)

#### **Main Hall (National Important Cultural Property)**

The main hall boasts an overwhelming sense of dignity with its vermilion lacquer, gold leaf decorations, and unique roof structure. Make a wish as you pay your respects at the Betsugu and the Left and Right Palaces in order.

#### **Shiogama-zakura (Natural Monument)**

Within the shrine grounds, you can find the rare cherry blossom, **'Shiogama-zakura,'** which can only be seen here. Its spectacular appearance, with 30 to 50 overlapping petals, is truly a sight to behold. If you visit in late April to early May, be sure to see it.

#### **The Mystical Cauldrons (Okama Shrine)**

A short distance from the main shrine is the sub-shrine **'Okama Shrine,'** which houses the **'Four Divine Cauldrons'** said to have been used by the god in the myth to make salt. Legend has it that the water in the cauldrons never runs dry and its color changes when a calamity is about to befall the country. (This is considered one of Japan's Three Great Miracles!)

### 4. üç£ Nearby Attractions: A City of Sushi and Scenic Views

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the flavors and beauty of Miyagi.

#### **1. Shiogama Seafood Wholesale Market**

- **Location**: 5 minutes by car, 15 minutes on foot.
- **Features**: A paradise of fresh seafood! The **'My Kaisen-don'** (make-your-own seafood bowl), where you buy small portions of freshly caught tuna, scallops, and sea urchin to place on a bowl of rice, is a must-try.

#### **2. Sushi Street**

- **Features**: Shiogama has the highest number of sushi restaurants per capita in Japan. Being right next to the port, freshness is a given! Savor the **premium sushi** crafted by master chefs.

#### **3. Matsushima, one of Japan's Three Great Views**

- **Access**: 10 minutes by train (to Matsushima-Kaigan Station).
- **Features**: Enjoy the stunning view of Matsushima Bay with its 260 islands on a cruise boat. Don't miss the National Treasure Zuigan-ji Temple and Godaido Hall with its iconic red bridge.
