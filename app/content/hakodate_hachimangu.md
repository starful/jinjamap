#### **„ÄêOrigin and History: The Guardian Deity of Hakodate„Äë**

In 1445, an old man appeared in a dream to the local lord, Kono Masamichi, and delivered a revelation: "If you enshrine me in this place, it will prosper forever." The small shrine he built became the origin of Hakodate Hachimangu Shrine.
Since then, the city has faced numerous trials, including wars and fires, but the shrine has stood firm, becoming a spiritual pillar for its citizens. In 1880, it was moved to its current location in Yachigashira, becoming the true **'Sochinju of Hakodate (guardian deity of the entire city)'**.

### 2. üå≤ A Stroll Through the Precincts: A Breathtaking View from the Hilltop

The view of the shrine that greets you after climbing the steep hill is moving in itself. Here are the points you shouldn't miss.

#### The Haiden and Otori-zukuri Architecture

At the end of the approach, the magnificent Haiden (hall of worship) appears. Its unique **'Otori-zukuri'** style, with a sharply peaked roof, boasts a rare architectural beauty seldom seen even in Japan. Offer your prayers here with reverence, following the 'two bows, two claps, one bow' method.

#### Look Behind You! The Stunning View of Hakodate Bay

Once you reach the top of the stone steps, be sure to turn around. Beyond the straight-stretching path, you can see the city of Hakodate and the blue sea (Tsugaru Strait) in a single glance. It is the best viewpoint to experience the perspective of the deity watching over the city.

#### Power Spots: The Dream-Granting Water and the Old Pagoda Tree

- **Yumemi-no-mizu (Dream-Viewing Water)**: A mystical well tied to the shrine's founding legend. It is said that your dreams and goals will come true if you touch or drink this water.
- **Enju-no-ki (Japanese Pagoda Tree)**: This ancient sacred pagoda tree is believed to ward off evil spirits (È≠î) and promote longevity. Touch the tree and feel its powerful life force.

### 4. ‚ô®Ô∏è Nearby Attractions: Hot Springs and Soul Food

After visiting the shrine, enjoy the true charms of Hakodate.

#### **1. Yachigashira Onsen**

- **Location**: Just below the shrine (5-minute walk).
- **Features**: A public hot spring loved by Hakodate citizens. The iron-rich, brown-colored water warms the body to the core. Soaking in the open-air bath while gazing at Mt. Hakodate is pure healing! (Remember to bring your own towel).

#### **2. Hakodate Park**

- **Location**: Next to the shrine (3-minute walk).
- **Features**: One of Japan's first Western-style parks. With a small zoo and a retro amusement park, it's a great place for a stroll. It is also famous as a cherry blossom spot in the spring.

#### **3. Hasegawa Store (Yakitori Bento)**

- **Recommended Menu**: Try Hakodate's soul food, the **'Yakitori Bento'**. Although its name means grilled chicken skewers, it's actually a bento box with grilled pork skewers. The savory flavor of the skewers, grilled to order, is incredibly addictive! (The cooking counter is inside the convenience store).
