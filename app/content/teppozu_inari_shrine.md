**Teppozu Inari Shrine (ÈêµÁ†≤Ê¥≤Á®≤Ëç∑Á•ûÁ§æ)** is not just a neighborhood shrine; it is a venerable institution that has witnessed the transformation of Japan from the early Heian period to the modern era. Established in **841 AD**, it is one of the oldest spiritual sites in central Tokyo.

**The Meaning Behind the Name 'Teppozu'**
The name "Teppozu" (Gun-shaped Sandbar) predates the introduction of firearms to Japan. It originally described the unique shape of the sandbank at the mouth of the river where the shrine was first located. This gun-shaped strip of land was a critical landmark for sailors entering the heart of Edo. As the city grew through land reclamation, the shrine was relocated several times, finally settling at its current location in the Minato district during the Meiji era.

**A Trinity of Protection**
The shrine enshrines a powerful combination of deities:
1.  **Ukanomitama-no-mikoto (Inari):** The deity of rice and life-force, worshipped for business success.
2.  **Kotohira-no-kami (Kompira):** The supreme protector of maritime travel and seafaring.
3.  **Sukunabikona-no-kami:** The deity of medicine, healing, and brewing.

In the Edo period, Teppozu Inari was the final spiritual stop for ships entering the capital. Captains of the "Tarumansen" (sake barrel ships) and "Higaki-kaisen" (cargo ships) would offer prayers here, thanking the gods for a safe voyage across the treacherous seas.

### 3. üìú Goshuin and Shrine Items: The Spirit of the Cold Water Rite

**The Kanchu Misogi Heritage**
Teppozu Inari is famous for its **Kanchu Misogi** (Mid-winter Cold Water Purification) held every January. Worshipers in traditional loincloths bathe in freezing water to purify their souls and pray for health. The shrine‚Äôs **Goshuin** (holy stamp) often carries the weight of this tradition, featuring bold, powerful calligraphy. During the month of January or on Mt. Fuji-related festival days, you may receive a special stamp depicting the Fujizuka or the purification rite.

**Amulets for the Modern Traveler**
Given the presence of Kotohira-no-kami, the amulets for **Travel Safety** (*Kotsu Anzen*) are highly regarded. Many frequent flyers and sea travelers visit here to receive a charm to ensure they "return to port" safely.
