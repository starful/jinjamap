Behind the grandeur of Heian Jingu lies the story of two emperors who bookend Kyoto's history.

This shrine is dedicated to the **50th Emperor Kanmu** and the **121st Emperor Komei**.

- **Emperor Kanmu**: In 794, he moved the capital from Nara to Kyoto (Heian-kyo), laying the foundation for the thousand-year capital. He symbolizes the **'beginning'** of Kyoto and is revered as a deity who brings **good fortune (kaiun)** and helps with **new beginnings**.
- **Emperor Komei**: He was the last emperor to be enthroned in Kyoto. A figure who dedicated himself to the nation's peace during the turbulent end of the shogunate, he symbolizes the **'end'** of imperial Kyoto and is believed to grant blessings for **peace** and **matchmaking**.

#### **ã€Origin and History: Glory Revived by the Power of the Peopleã€‘**

The year 1895 marked the 1100th anniversary of Kyoto becoming the capital. At the time, Kyoto was losing its vitality after the emperor's departure. Feeling a sense of crisis, the citizens of Kyoto organized an exposition to revive the city and founded Heian Jingu as its centerpiece.
This shrine, a 5/8 scale replica of the **'Chodo-in' (æœå ‚é™¢)**, the center of government during the Heian period, is a monumental place embodying the citizens' determination to revive Kyoto's past glory and move toward the future.

The moment you step into the precincts, you'll feel as if you've traveled back in time to the Heian period. Here are the must-see points.

#### **O-torii and Otenmon Gate**

The giant red torii gate that straddles the road in front of the shrine stands at an impressive 24.4 meters. Passing through this gate, you'll see the majestic two-story **'Otenmon Gate' (å¿œå¤©é–€)**. The harmony of its blue tiles and vermilion pillars is the epitome of splendor.

#### **Daigokuden (å¤§æ¥µæ®¿)**

Beyond the Otenmon Gate, a vast white gravel courtyard unfolds, with the **'Daigokuden'** standing at its end. This was the main hall where important state ceremonies were held, flanked by towers symbolizing the Azure Dragon and the White Tiger. Offer your prayers at the front hall (Haiden) with the 'two bows, two claps, one bow' ritual and absorb its majestic energy.

#### **The Secret Garden, Shin-en (ç¥è‹‘)**

The highlight of Heian Jingu! This is a vast garden of approximately 33,000 square meters that envelops the area behind the main shrine buildings.

- **Spring**: It is a prime cherry blossom spot, famous for its weeping red cherry trees in full bloom.
- **Summer**: The pond is filled with irises and water lilies, showing off their pure beauty.
- **Autumn & Winter**: The serene scenery created by autumn leaves and snowscapes brings peace to the mind.
  In particular, the view from the **'Taihei-kaku'** bridge, which crosses the pond in the East Garden (Higashi Shin-en), offers an unforgettable, moving experience. (Separate admission fee required)

### 4. ğŸµ Nearby Attractions: Okazaki, a District of Art and Cuisine

The Okazaki area, where the shrine is located, is the cultural and artistic heart of Kyoto.

#### **1. Kyoto City KYOCERA Museum of Art**

- **Location**: Right next to the O-torii gate.
- **Features**: One of the oldest public art museum buildings in Japan. It has recently been renovated and reborn as a hot spot where classical and modern beauty coexist.

#### **2. Nanzen-ji Temple & Suirokaku Aqueduct**

- **Access**: 15-minute walk.
- **Features**: A temple famous for its impressive red-brick arched aqueduct. It's a perfect course for a tranquil stroll.

#### **3. Yudofu (Tofu) Restaurant 'Okabeya'**

- **Location**: Near Nanzen-ji Temple.
- **Menu**: The tofu dish **'Yudofu'**, made with Kyoto's clear water, is mild and delicate, making it easy on the stomach. You can enjoy your meal while looking out at a beautiful garden.
