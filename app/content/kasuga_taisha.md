The mystique of Kasuga Taisha is special from its very beginning.

#### **ã€Enshrined Deities (å¾¡ç¥­ç¥): A Powerful Ensembleã€‘**

This is the head shrine of the 'Kasuga-no-kami,' enshrining a remarkable four deities.

- **Takemikazuchi**: The god of war who descended on a white deer. He is responsible for **national protection**, **victory**, and **warding off evil**.
- **Futsunushi**: The god of swords, who ensures **success** and **traffic safety**.
- **Amenokoyane**: The god of wisdom and words, who aids in **academic achievement** and **career advancement**.
- **Himegami**: The goddess who presides over love and peace, assisting with **marital harmony** and **matchmaking**.

With such powerful deities gathered here, Kasuga Taisha is known as an all-purpose power spot that can grant any of life's wishes.

#### **ã€Origins and History: Why Deer Are Considered Sacredã€‘**

In 768, the Fujiwara clan needed a guardian deity for the capital city of Nara. They decided to invite the god Takemikazuchi from the distant 'Kashima Jingu' in Ibaraki Prefecture. It was then that the legend was born: the god descended to the summit of Mount Mikasa, riding a **white deer** through the clouds.

From that time on, Nara became the 'mountain where the gods dwell,' and the deer, as 'messengers of the gods,' received utmost protection. It is likely thanks to this millennium of belief that the deer in Nara Park today are not afraid of people and allow them to come close.

### 2. ğŸ¦Œ A Stroll Through the Grounds: The Forest of Lanterns and the Shrine of Matchmaking

The shrine grounds are a magical space where simply walking around is a healing experience. Here are some points you shouldn't miss.

#### **The Path of Stone Lanterns and the Main Sanctuary**

Along both sides of the approach, over 2,000 moss-covered stone lanterns stretch endlessly. These were dedicated one by one by people of the past making wishes, eventually forming a forest of their own. At the end of the path, the vermilion Main Sanctuary and its corridors appear in perfect harmony with nature. The 1,000 bronze lanterns hanging in the corridors are particularly breathtaking.

#### **Fujinami-no-ya (Lantern Experience in the Dark)**

The highlight of Kasuga Taisha! This is a dark room where you can experience the magic of the **'Mantoro Festival,'** held only twice a year in February and August, all year round. The hundreds of flickering lights in the pitch-black darkness offer a dreamlike experience, as if you are floating through the cosmos. It's also a famous spot for taking unforgettable photos.

#### **Meoto Daikokusha Shrine**

This is the only shrine in Japan that enshrines a **married couple of deities**. The ema (votive plaques) here are shaped like pink hearts! It's a power spot overflowing with love, constantly visited by people praying for **marital bliss** and **success in love**. Don't forget to try the 'Mizu-ura Mikuji,' a water fortune-telling where you float a heart-shaped ema.

#### **Wakamiya JÅ«go-sha Meguri (Pilgrimage of 15 Shrines)**

This is a pilgrimage course that takes you to 15 smaller shrines along a forest path near the main sanctuary. Meet various deities who grant different wishes for health, wisdom, and wealth, and soak up the pure energy of the forest.
