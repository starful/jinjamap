The main deity (ä¸»ç¥­ç¥) here is **Takeiwatatsu-no-Mikoto**, the grandson of Japan's first emperor.
As his name, meaning 'strong as a rock and powerful as a dragon,' suggests, he was the hero who pioneered the Aso region. According to legend, he drained the water from the Aso caldera, which was originally a giant lake, and turned it into fertile land where people could live.
For this reason, he is revered as the **'God of Pioneering'** and the **'God of Agriculture'**, and has become a steadfast guardian deity who watches over **business prosperity**, **new beginnings**, and **family safety**.

#### **ã€Origins and History: 2,500 Years Beyond Disasterã€‘**

Aso Shrine is an ancient shrine (å¤ç¤¾) of profound history, said to have been founded in 281 BC. As the 'Ichinomiya (first-ranked shrine)' of Higo Province (Kumamoto), it has served as the spiritual pillar of the region for 2,500 years.
It experienced the tragedy of its National Treasure-class Romon gate and worship hall collapsing in the 2016 earthquake. However, restoration work is underway with nationwide support, and the process itself has become a symbol of 'revival' and 'hope.' (The Romon gate was completely restored in 2023!)

Aso Shrine has unique points of worship hidden within its grounds. Don't miss a single one!

#### **Yokosando and the Majestic Romon Gate**

Unlike most shrines where the main path leads directly to the main hall, Aso Shrine's approach is a **'Yokosando'**, which runs alongside the main hall. The massive Romon gate (æ¨“é–€) boasts an overwhelming presence, considered one of the three great Romon gates of Japan. Feel its resilient energy as you stand before the gate that has overcome the earthquake.

#### **Takasago-no-Matsu (The Pine of Marriage)**

Beside the worship hall stands a pine tree called **'Takasago-no-Matsu'**. It is said that walking around this tree will lead you to a good partner.

- **Men**: Walk two circles to the left.
- **Women**: Walk two circles to the right.
  Take a slow walk around it while making a wish.

#### **Negaikakeishi (Wish-Granting Stone)**

This mystical stone within the precincts is the **'Wish-Granting Stone'**. It is believed that if you state your wish three times in your heart and then stroke the stone three times, your wish will come true. You can feel the earnest desires of countless people from the stone's smooth, polished surface.

### 4. ğŸ’§ Nearby Attractions: Monzen-machi, Where Clear Water Flows

In front of the shrine, a charming shopping street unfolds. The main attraction here is **'water'**.

#### **1. Mizuki (Water Source) Tour**

- **Features**: Throughout the shopping street, there are **'Mizuki (water spots)'** where clear spring water gushes out. Each has a different name, such as 'Water of Learning' or 'Water of Financial Luck,' so enjoy a stroll while comparing the tastes of the water. It's a good idea to bring a tumbler!

#### **2. Akaushi (Red Cow) Gourmet**

- **Recommendation**: Be sure to try Aso's specialty, **'Akaushi-don'** (red cow rice bowl) or **'Akaushi croquettes'**. The beef, which is low in fat, light, and full of juicy flavor, will give you an unforgettable taste experience.

#### **3. Mt. Aso Nakadake Crater**

- **Access**: 30 minutes by car.
- **Features**: This is the active volcano that serves as the backdrop for the myths. Witness the awe of nature as you gaze upon the bubbling, emerald-green caldera lake. (Access may be restricted depending on volcanic activity.)
